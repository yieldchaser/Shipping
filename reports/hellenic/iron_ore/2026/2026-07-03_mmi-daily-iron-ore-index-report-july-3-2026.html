MMI Daily Iron Ore Index Report July 3 2026

Hellenic Shipping News  |  03 July 2026  |  https://www.hellenicshippingnews.com/mmi-daily-iron-ore-index-report-july-3-2026

Today, iron ore futures on the DCE traded weaker, with contract 12609 finally closing at 734 yuan/ton,down 1.74% from the previous trading day. Port spot prices fell by 3-8 yuan/ton from the prior day. Trader activity was moderate, while steel mill purchases were mainly for replenishment; spot market transaction volumes have been average so far.

According to SMM surveys, iron ore inventory at 35 major ports nationwide this week stood at 148.30 million tons, down 36,000 tons week-on-week, indicating a slight overall destocking. Meanwhile, average daily outbound shipments from ports rose by 68,000 tons to 3.298 million tons, reflecting relatively sound short-term demand. Next week, arriving shipments are expected to increase further, tilting resource supply toward looser conditions; on the demand side, as hot metal output gradually declines, outbound shipment volumes are likely to follow suit. Support for iron ore prices is weakening, but n the near term, prices are likely to fluctuate within a range, as supply and demand dynamics remain largely in line with market expectations.
