MMI Daily Iron Ore Index Report July 23 2026

Hellenic Shipping News  |  23 July 2026  |  https://www.hellenicshippingnews.com/mmi-daily-iron-ore-index-report-july-23-2026

Today, the DCE iron ore futures moved strongly today. The most-traded DCE I2609 contract closed at 747.5 yuan/mt, up 0.74%. Spot prices at Qingdao Portrose roughly 5-8 yuan/mt from the previous trading day. Trader activity was moderate, and inquiries from steel mills were limited. Spot trading was poor. Recently, steel mills’ demand for iron ore has slowed. According to SMM data, inventories at the ten major ports totaled 105.63 million mt, down only 640,000 mt WoW. Based on current inventory structure, concentrate and lump ore destocking was relatively large, pellet inventories were at low levels, while fines saw a slight buildup. Demand side, off-season conditions persisted. Although environmental protection-driven production restrictions were relatively modest, the likelihood of steel mills voluntarily conducting blast furnace maintenance still exists. Next week’s Politburo meeting expectations are heating up, potentially boosting market sentiment in the short term. On the whole, however, iron ore prices are expected to continue to move sideways in the near term.
