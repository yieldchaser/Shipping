MMI Daily Iron Ore Index Report June 15 2026

Hellenic Shipping News  |  15 June 2026  |  https://www.hellenicshippingnews.com/mmi-daily-iron-ore-index-report-june-15-2026

The iron ore DCE contract fluctuated with a slight upward bias today, with the I2609 contract finallyclosing at 771.5 yuan/ton, up 0.72% from the previous trading session. Port spot prices rose by 3-5 yuan/ton from the previous day. Traders were active in quoting, but steel mills became more cautious and showed weaker purchasing willingness. The market transaction atmosphere was subdued, with relatively low spot trading volumes so far. The signing of a memorandum of understanding between the US and Iran, expected to be formally finalized on the 19th, is likely to bring an end to the conflict. This is positive for the global economy, and iron ore prices strengthened accordingly. However, from a fundamental perspective, last week’s SMM shipping data showed that shipment volumes from Australia and Brazil increased due to an end-of-quarter rush, while shipments from non-mainstream countries such as South Africa and India also rose. The resulting supply-side increase in iron ore connues to cap upside space. Additionally, with the implementation of the seventh round of coke price cuts today, losses have deepened for steel mills in isome regions. Combined with weak off-season demand, some steel companies may schedule blast furnace maintenance in advance, leading to expectations of lower iron ore demand. Overall, iron ore faces significant upward resistance and may maintain a narrow range-bound fluctuation in the short term.
