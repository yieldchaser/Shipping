MMI Daily Iron Ore Index Report July 13 2026

Hellenic Shipping News  |  13 July 2026  |  https://www.hellenicshippingnews.com/mmi-daily-iron-ore-index-report-july-13-2026

Today, iron ore prices on the Dalian Commodity Exchange (DCE) came under pressure, with the main contract I2609 finally settling at 744.5 yuan/ton,down approximately 0.47% from the previous session. Port spot prices fell by about 4-6 yuan/ton from the previous trading day. Traders’ quotation enthusiasm weakened, and steel mills’ purchases were mostly for replenishment needs. As of now, spot transaction volumes have been relatively low. As of last Friday, global iron ore total shipments reached 30.381 million tons, a decrease of 14% month-on-month. Among them, shipments from both Australia and Brazil saw significant reductions. During the same period, iron ore arrivals at Chinese ports were 25.015 million tons, down 13% month-on-month. The supply side of iron ore has tightened in the short term, but given the high port inventory level (147 million tons), the overall supply side remains relatively loose. As pig iron production enters a downward trend, iron ore demand is expected to weaken further. Therefore, from a fundamental perspective, iron ore prices are likely to fluctuate within a narrow range this week, with a tendency to decline.
