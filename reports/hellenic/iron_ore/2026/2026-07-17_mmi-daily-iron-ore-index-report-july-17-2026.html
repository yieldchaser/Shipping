MMI Daily Iron Ore Index Report July 17 2026

Hellenic Shipping News  |  17 July 2026  |  https://www.hellenicshippingnews.com/mmi-daily-iron-ore-index-report-july-17-2026

The iron ore market traded sideways today, with the DCE main contract I2609 eventually closing at 762 RMB/ton, up 0.53% from the previous day’sclosing price. Spot prices mostly followed with gains of 0–2 RMB/ton. Traders sold goods in line with market conditions; as the weekend approached, steel mills showed weaker purchasing willingness, and market trading activity was generally moderate.As of July 17, the latest SMM statistics show that iron ore inventory across 35 major ports nationwide totaled 145.13 million tonnes, down 2.03 million tonnes from the previous period, marking another round of destocking. During the same period, the average daily port oŏake fell slightly by 55,000 tonnes to 3.22 million tonnes. This week, due to short-term weather disruptions, arrivals at some ports dropped notably, and resource replenishment tightened significantly. Looking ahead to next week, although iron ore demand is expected to weaken further, the US-Iran conflict and longterm contract negotiations will continue to create disturbances in the market. Prices are expected to maintain a range-bound fluctuation next week.
