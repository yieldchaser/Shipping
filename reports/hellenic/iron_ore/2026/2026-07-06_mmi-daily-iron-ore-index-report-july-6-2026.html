MMI Daily Iron Ore Index Report July 6 2026

Hellenic Shipping News  |  06 July 2026  |  https://www.hellenicshippingnews.com/mmi-daily-iron-ore-index-report-july-6-2026

Today, DCE iron ore futures trended strong but pulled back slightly in the afternoon.Contract 12609 closed at 738 yuan/mt, up 0.14% from the previous trading day. Port spot prices rose 2-3 yuan/mt from the previous day. Traders were active in quoting, steel mills purchased as needed, inquiry interest was moderate, with spot transaction volumes remaining low

Looking ahead, as the downstream enters the off-season and the pace of mine shipments slows, the supply side of iron ore is expected to tighten slightly, though the overall loose pattern will be hard to change. Last week, SMM’s global iron ore shipments totaled 35.16 million mt, up 1.53% WoW, with cumulative YoY growth of 1.77%. Among them, Australia’s shipments edged down 6% WoW, while Brazil’s surged nearly 20% WoW, and non-mainstream countries’ shipments edged up slightly WoW. Among the four major miners, shipments from Rio Tinto and FMG declined. In addition, last week, SMM’s China iron ore arrivals totaled 28.78 million mt, edging down 1.88% WoW, with cumulative YoY growth of 6.07%. Overall, iron ore prices are likely to fluctuate within a range in the short term, while the long-term outlook remains bearish.
