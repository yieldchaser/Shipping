MMI Daily Iron Ore Index Report July 2 2026

Hellenic Shipping News  |  02 July 2026  |  https://www.hellenicshippingnews.com/mmi-daily-iron-ore-index-report-july-2-2026

Today’s DCE iron ore futures showed weakness after rallying in the night session.Contract I2609 settled at 740 yuan/ton, up 0.48% from the previous trading day. Port spot prices rose 5-8 yuan/ton versus the prior session. Trader activity was moderate, with steel mills procuring on an as-needed basis; spot trading volumes have remained unremarkable so
far.

The iron ore supply-demand fundamentals remain largely stable, with supply on the loose side. Going forward, the pace of mine shipments is expected to slow, keeping the overall
picture relatively steady. On the demand side, Southeast Asian steel exports have entered the oﬀ-season, prompting regional traders to actively cut prices to compete for market
share. As a result, domestic export demand has begun to soften. However, domestic steel inventories still have buﬀer room, and this is unlikely to trigger production-cut intentions
among steel mills in the near term. On the news front, aside from supply-tightening rumors for speciﬁc varieties, there are no events decisive enough to drive market direction.

Taken together, iron ore prices are expected to trade in a range-bound pattern in the short term.
