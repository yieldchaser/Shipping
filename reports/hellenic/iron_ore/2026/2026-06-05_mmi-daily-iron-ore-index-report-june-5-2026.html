MMI Daily Iron Ore Index Report June 5 2026

Hellenic Shipping News  |  05 June 2026  |  https://www.hellenicshippingnews.com/mmi-daily-iron-ore-index-report-june-5-2026

The DCE iron ore futures trended weaker, with the most-traded contract I2609 finally closing at766 yuan/mt, down 0.91% from the previous trading session. Port spot prices fell 1-5 yuan/mt from the previous day. Traders’ quotng interest was not high; steel mills mainly restocked as needed, with few inquiries; as of now, spot transactions were limited. Currently, steel mill procurement pace has started to slow down. According to the latest SMM data, the daily average port pick-up volume at 35 main ports across the country edged down 37,000 mt to 3.2 million mt, showing signs of peaking and pulling back from the high in May. Over the same period, inventories at main ports were 148.39 million mt, up 440,000 mt MoM, marking the first increase since the end of March. Moreover, the fifth round of coke price increases was implemented today, while expectations for a sixth-round increase remain. Squeezed steel mill profits may force ore prices to give way. Therefore, considering all factors, iron ore prices struggle to rise in the short term, and the trend may be predominantly in the doldrums.
