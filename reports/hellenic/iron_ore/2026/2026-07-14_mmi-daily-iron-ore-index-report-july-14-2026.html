MMI Daily Iron Ore Index Report July 14 2026

Hellenic Shipping News  |  14 July 2026  |  https://www.hellenicshippingnews.com/mmi-daily-iron-ore-index-report-july-14-2026

Today, the iron ore market showed relatively strong performance on the Dalian Commodity Exchange (DCE), with the benchmark contractI2609 finally closing at 760.5 yuan/ton, up 1.81%. Qingdao port spot prices remained firm, rising by approximately 10–12 yuan/ton from the previous trading day. Traders became more active, while steel mills, apart from rigid replenishment, showed generally low purchasing willingness. Spot transaction volumes have remained modest so far. In terms of mill maintenance schedules, iron ore demand is expected to decline further. According to SMM statistics, the blast furnace maintenance-induced hot metal impact for this week (July 11–July 17) is 1.3434 million tons, an increase of 70,100 tons from the previous week. For next week (July 18–July 24), the impact is projected to reach 1.4109 million tons, up by 67,500 tons week-on-week. On the news front, the strike at Port Hedland and the upcoming shipping restrictions are likely to tighten supply side in the short term. Taken together, iron ore prices may see a modest bottom rebound in the near term, but are still expected to remain within a range-bound fluctuation pattern overall.
