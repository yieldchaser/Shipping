Weekly Vessel Valuations Report, June 16 2026

Hellenic Shipping News  |  16 June 2026  |  https://www.hellenicshippingnews.com/weekly-vessel-valuations-report-june-16-2026

Bulkers:Bulker secondhand activity this week was dominated by Chinese buyers. The concentration of Chinese buyinginterest across multiple size segments points to sustained regional demand. Bulker values remained stable this week.

Capesize Kerkis (176,900 DWT, 2007, Namura) sold to Chinese SS/DD Due for USD 25.5 mil, VV Value USD 25.3 mil.

Kamsarmax Themis (81,900 DWT, 2012, COSCO Dalian) sold by Lavinia Bulk for USD 18 mil, VV Value USD 18.2 mil.

Supramax AE Mars (53,600 DWT, 2006, Yangzhou Dayang) sold to Chinese Auction SS/DD Due for USD 9.05 mil, VV Value USD 10.1 mil.

Handysize Woohyun Sky (32,300 DWT, 2010, Samho Tongyoung) sold to Chinese for USD 10.5 mil, VV Value USD 11.2 mil.

Tankers:Values firmed across crude sectors this week, with older tonnage Aframax Tankers leading gains despite the recent decline in earnings. A Hormuz resolution is expected to sharpen appetite for modern tonnage, with fleet renewal demand set to accelerate as sanctioned and ageing vessels are phased out.

VLCC Maxim (296,900 DWT, Jun 2011, Jiangnan Shanghai Changxing HI) sold to Sinokor for USD 82 mil, VV Value USD 80.38 mil.

Suezmax Brugge & Brest (156,900-157,100 DWT, 2023, Hyundai Samho HI) sold by CMB Tech in an en bloc deal for USD 220 mil, VV Value USD 219.96 mil.

Containers:A few more sales have come to light this week, but with only a small number to choose from buyers are taking vessels with TCs and for forward delivery to ensure they have the capacity they need.

Feedermax Alexander (966 TEU, Nov 2006, Yangfan Zhoushan) sold for USD 11.5 mil to European buyers, VV Value USD 9.79 mil.

Panamax HT Capricorn (5,100 TEU, Jun 2010, Jingnan Shanghai) sold Inc TC for USD 48 mil, VV Value USD 43.2 mil.
