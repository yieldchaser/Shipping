Weekly Vessel Valuations Report, April 21 2026

Hellenic Shipping News  |  21 April 2026  |  https://www.hellenicshippingnews.com/weekly-vessel-valuations-report-april-21-2026

Bulkers:Bulker secondhand market activity remained healthy this week, with transactions recorded across a spread of sizes.Appetite from Chinese buyers dominated the week’s activity, with Supramaxes proving particularly liquid. Despite uncertainty surrounding the Strait of Hormuz, investor appetite for quality secondhand tonnage has shown little sign of cooling. Values were mixed; firmer pricing drove an upward revision in the Panamax and Handysize sectors, while mid-age Supramax values softened in line with evidence of weaker pricing.

Panamaxes Seacon Vancouver (85,700 DWT, 2023, GSW) and Seacon Oslo (85,500 DWT, 2023, GSW) sold by Seacon Shipping Group for USD 72 mil, VV Value USD 72.6 mil

Panamax Skyros (79,400 DWT, 2011, Nanjing Wujiazui) sold by Sea Traders for USD 13.95 mil, VV Value USD 13.1 mil

Supramax Serene Amelia (57,700 DWT, 2010, STX Offshore) sold by Chandris for USD 14.5 mil, VV Value USD 15.9 mil

Handysize Astro Orion (37,300 DWT, 2017, Jinling Weihai) sold to Cosmoship for USD 21 mil, VV Value USD 18.6 mil

Tankers:VV Tanker values remained broadly stable this week amid a slowdown in S&P activity. Older LR1 tonnage continued to firm following a recent en bloc deal.

LR1 PM Imperial & PM Monarch (c.76,500 DWT, 2007, Dalian Shipbuilding Industry Corp) sold by Quantum Global for USD 40 mil en bloc, VV Value USD 38.08 mil

MR2 GT Freedom (46,000 DWT, May 2003, Shin Kurushima Onishi) sold DD Due by Allsea DMCC JSC for USD 8.5 mil, VV Value USD 9.68 mil

MR1 (Chemical/Product) East Coast (37,500 DWT, Mar 2005, Hyundai Mipo) sold to Unknown Greek buyers for USD 11.7 mil, VV Value USD 12.14 mil

Containers:Majority of container values remained stable this week, some small adjustments for handy containers following the most recent orders.

No notable sales.
