GMS Week 24 – PREMIUM CRACKS, PEN HOVERS

Hellenic Shipping News  |  13 June 2026  |  https://www.hellenicshippingnews.com/best-oasis-weekly-recycling-market-report-05-june-2026

The war premium has finally cracked. Brent crude fell more than 4% on Thursday toward USD 89 per barrel, its lowest level since March,with WTI near USD 86, after President Trump suspended military strikes against Iran that had been scheduled for that evening and told reporters that a deal reopening the Strait of Hormuz could be signed as early as this weekend, likely in Europe. Iran’s semi-official Fars news agency reported that Tehran is likely to approve the agreement, though no final text has been confirmed by either side. The Weekly first reported the war premium cracking in Week 15. Nine weeks later, the premium has finally read the report.

The pen is hovering, not yet landed. The week that produced the breakthrough also produced US strikes on Iranian radar and drone sites on June 10 and Iranian missile responses across the Gulf, with mediation now running through Qatar and draft texts still being exchanged. The framework requires Iran to clear all mines from Hormuz within 30 days of signature and bars tolls on the strait, while even a signed deal faces idled production fields, damaged export facilities, and the slow mechanics of restoring confidence to a waterway that has spent a quarter as a war zone. The agreement could be signed this weekend, in Europe, which is at least a new continent for the deadline to miss.

Freight has cooled for the first time in the arc. The Baltic Dry Index eased to 2,818 on June 9 from the June 1 peak of 3,222, with the Baltic Capesize Index at 4,441 and daily Capesize earnings at USD 40,274, down from the USD 49,511 high of the prior week, though Supramax and Handysize both firmed. For the first time since February, the two forces that have kept older tonnage trading rather than beaching, the bunker-cost floor and the freight earnings premium, are softening in the same week. They have chosen the week the beach is closed for the season. The market’s timing remains undefeated.

May inflation prints have landed across the basin, and the pattern is uniform: annual rates climbing on accumulated base effects even as the monthly shocks fade. Pakistan’s May CPI rose to 11.7%, the highest since June 2024, yet monthly inflation collapsed to 0.5% from 2.5%. Turkey’s May CPI edged to 32.61% while its monthly pace fell to 1.71% from 4.18%. The fire is going out at the stove while the smoke alarm still rings. Currencies extended their repair: the Indian Rupee held its recovery near 95.40, the Pakistani Rupee sustained its 2026 best at approximately 278.39, the Bangladeshi Taka held the floor of its band at 122.75, and the Turkish Lira steadied near its record at approximately 45.85. The monsoon now rules the beaching calendar regardless of what is signed in Europe this weekend. But the shape of the next chapter is forming: if the pen lands, the 30-day de-mining clock starts, the strait normalises through July, the bunker floor and freight premium continue to deflate, and the tonnage that nine weeks of war could not release begins to face the question the war deferred. The first chapter of this arc recorded one unbroken fact: the ships did not come. The second chapter opens with the first credible reason to believe they eventually will, arriving, as everything in this market has arrived, precisely when the beach cannot take them. The premium cracks. The pen hovers. The monsoon rules.
