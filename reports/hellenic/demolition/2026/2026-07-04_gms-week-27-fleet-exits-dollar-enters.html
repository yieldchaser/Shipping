GMS Week 27 – FLEET EXITS, DOLLAR ENTERS

Hellenic Shipping News  |  04 July 2026  |  https://www.hellenicshippingnews.com/gms-week-27-fleet-exits-dollar-enters

The war is over, and the fleet is finally moving. Crude flows through Hormuz exceeded 10 million barrels per daythis week under US military coordination, while Lloyd’s List counts roughly 550 merchant vessels preparing to exit the Gulf, including some 200 bulk carriers. The central channel remains constrained by an estimated 80 mines, keeping traffic to the northern and southern routes for now, but the direction of travel is no longer theoretical. Tehran continues to decline face-to-face talks with the Americans in Doha. Its cargoes carry no such reservations.

Brent has settled near USD 71 per barrel, effectively back at its pre-war level, closing the quarter with its sharpest fall since 2020 after touching USD 126.41 on April 30. Analysts have cut their 2026 forecasts for the first time since the war began, after five consecutive months of raising them, even as US crude inventories have now drawn for twelve straight weeks. The barrel has completed the round trip. The inventory shortage remains. The price has chosen the reopening.

Freight has adjusted, but not collapsed. The Baltic Dry Index bounced late to approximately 2,650 on iron ore strength, with Capesize earnings near USD 32,060 per day. The index gained 25% for the quarter and lost 22% for the month, which is the cleanest measure yet of the conflict’s afterlife in freight. The war’s freight premium outlived the war by exactly one month. It is now fading into a market where the ships that stayed trading through the conflict have fewer reasons to keep doing so.

The dollar has taken the war’s place as the basin’s moving force. The Indian Rupee gave back part of its recovery to near 95.1, the Bangladeshi Taka eased through the top of its long-held band toward 123.2, and the Turkish Lira set another record low near 46.70. The Pakistani Rupee alone strengthened, breaking below 278, while Pakistan’s June CPI turned lower to 11.1%, with prices falling outright on the month. Islamabad’s July 1 tax and duty SROs have also quietly shifted the ground beneath Gadani, introducing a local-policy variable just as the external war premium disappears.

The yards now face two queues. One is the visible line of vessels exiting the Gulf. The other is the deferred recycling wave that the war held offshore and the monsoon is still holding from the beaches. The exit queue and the deflating premium govern what will come. The rains and the tide calendar govern when it can land. The ships are moving. The beaches are waiting. The war deferred the wave; the weather now decides its arrival.
