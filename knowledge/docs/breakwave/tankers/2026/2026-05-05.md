## Summary
The tanker shipping market is experiencing a divide between the Atlantic and Middle East regions due to geopolitical risks, particularly transit uncertainty through the Strait of Hormuz. VLCC rates are influenced more by access and risk than traditional supply-demand fundamentals, with the Atlantic absorbing displaced tonnage. Oil prices are projected to remain above $90/bbl due to structural deficits, while long-term tanker market fundamentals may face a downcycle as new vessel orders increase.
