## Summary
The VLCC spot rate in the Arabian Gulf has dropped from recent highs, driven by geopolitical risks and weakening Chinese demand, which has fallen to 6.4 million barrels per day. Despite global inventories remaining drawn down, crude prices have returned to pre-war levels due to a lack of appetite from Chinese refiners. Longer-term market outlook suggests a potential industry downcycle due to increased vessel ordering.
