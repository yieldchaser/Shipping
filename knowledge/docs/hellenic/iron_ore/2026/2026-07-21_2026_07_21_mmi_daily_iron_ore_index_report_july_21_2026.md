## Summary
Main
Today, the DCE iron ore futures trended weakly, with a slight recovery in the afternoon. The most-traded DCE I2609 contract closed at 749 yuan/mt,up 1.13%. Spot prices at Qingdao Port fell about 5-12 yuan/mt from the previous trading day.

## Main
Today, the DCE iron ore futures trended weakly, with a slight recovery in the afternoon. The most-traded DCE I2609 contract closed at 749 yuan/mt,up 1.13%. Spot prices at Qingdao Port fell about 5-12 yuan/mt from the previous trading day. Trader quoting enthusiasm was mediocre, and steel mill purchase willingness was mediocre. As of now, spot cargo trading volumes were mediocre. Currently, supply-side pressure in iron ore has not yet become prominent, and overall inventory remains in a destocking trend. As of last weekend, total iron ore inventory across 35 main ports nationwide stood at 145.13 million mt, down 2.03 million mt WoW; daily average port pick-up volume edged down 55,000 mt to 3.22 million mt. Meanwhile, blast furnace maintenance intensity increased further. According to SMM statistics, the hot metal output impact from blast furnace maintenance this week was 1.3584 million mt, up 67,500 mt WoW. Next week, the hot metal impact is expected to be 1.5097 million mt, up 151,300 mt from this week, and the demand side is therefore expected to continue to decline. Looking at the overall supply-demand fundamentals, iron ore prices are expected to consolidate on a subdued note in the short term
