## Main
On May 25, 2026, iron ore futures opened higher but retreated in the afternoon. The main contract I2609 finally closedat 793 yuan/ton, up 0.06% from the previous trading session. Port spot prices remained unchanged from the previous day. Trader quoting activity was moderate; steel mills purchased on a need-to basis with limited inquiries; overall spot market transactions were low so far today. Looking ahead, iron ore supply has increased in terms of shipments but narrowed slightly on the supply side. According to SMM statistics, last week's global shipments totalled 33.9581 million tonnes, up 9.82% month-on-month, but arrivals at Chinese ports totalled 24.31 million tonnes, down 6% month-on-month. The increase in shipments has yet to translate into arrivals, leaving near-port available resources tight on the demand side. Some blast furnaces under maintenance are scheduled to resume production this week, and both hot metal output and iron ore purchases are expected to rise in tandem. Given this near-term supply-demand mismatch, ore prices are likely to find support and trade higher in the short term.
