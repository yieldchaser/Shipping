## Main
The DCE iron ore futures market strengthened in a volatile manner today, with the main contract I2609 ultimately closingat 783.5 RMB/ton, up 0.45% from the previous trading session. Port spot prices rose slightly by 3-5 RMB/ton compared to yesterday. Traders showed moderate enthusiasm for quoting; steel mills purchased on a need-to basis; as the weekend approached, overall market trading activity was subdued. The transaction price for PB fines at Shandong ports was 754 RMB/ton. At Caofeidian Port, PB fines traded at 760 RMB/ton, while Jimblebar fines traded at 723 RMB/ton. According to today's SMM data, inventories at 35 ports continued to decline, but the pace of destocking has narrowed significantly, with the risk of inventory accumulation increasing steadily after June. Meanwhile, rising coke prices are squeezing steel mill margins, further weighing on iron ore prices. Additionally, weather-related factors have weakened end-user demand further, and market sentiment is leaning bearish. Given these combined effects, iron ore prices are expected to continue their narrow range-bound fluctuation, with the upper and lower bounds remaining unchanged.
