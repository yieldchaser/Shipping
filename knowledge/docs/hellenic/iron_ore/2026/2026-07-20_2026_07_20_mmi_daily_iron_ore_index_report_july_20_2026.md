## Main
Today, the DCE iron ore futures moved sideways today, with the most-traded DCE I2609 contract closing at 758 yuan/mt, edging down 0.39%from yesterday's closing price. Spot prices fell by 0-5 yuan/mt in tandem; traders sold based on market conditions; steel mills purchased as needed, and overall market trading sentiment on Monday was subdued. Supply side, total global iron ore shipments tracked by SMM last week reached 30.98 million mt, up 2% WoW, with cumulative growth of 1% YoY. Shipments from Australia and Brazil both edged up, but those from non-mainstream countries dropped notably WoW, while the four major miners posted a slight increase. Meanwhile, total iron ore arrivals at Chinese ports last week came to 19.75 million mt, down 21% WoW but with cumulative growth of 4% YoY, mainly affected by typhoons and heavy rains; a recovery is expected this week. Demand side, steel mill shipments fell further due to the weather, weakening their willingness to purchase raw materials and in turn weighing on spot prices. Overall, ore prices are expected to continue their sideways consolidation in the short term
