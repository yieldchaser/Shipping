## Main
Today, the DCE iron ore futures trended strong today, pulling back slightly near the close. The most-traded I2609contract closed at 748 yuan/ mt, up 0.81% from the previous trading session. Port spot prices rose 8-11 yuan/mt from the prior day. Trader quoting activity was moderate, steel mills maintained a strong wait-and-see stance, and spot trading volumes were moderate as of now. Looking ahead, iron ore port data for this period, according to an SMM survey, showed a slight destocking. Total inventory reached 148.66 million mt, down 690,000 mt MoM, while port pick-up volume edged down 38,000 mt to 3.23 million mt. The destocking was mainly driven by a decline in port arrivals. Overall, the iron ore supply side remained loose. On the news front, market rumors were frequent, and the tug-of-war between longs and shorts was fierce, with no clear divergence yet. With fundamentals and sentiment intertwined, ore prices are likely to continue moving sideways in the short term.
