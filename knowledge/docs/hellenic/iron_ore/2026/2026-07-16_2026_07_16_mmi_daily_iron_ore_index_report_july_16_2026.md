## Main
The iron ore market saw narrow fluctuations today, with the DCE main contract I2609 eventually closing at 759.5 RMB/ton,unchanged from the previous day. Spot prices at Qingdao Port fell by 2-5 RMB/ton compared to the previous trading day. Traders sold goods in line with market conditions, while steel mills showed improved purchasing willingness, and market trading activity was better than yesterday. Among them, the transaction price for PB fines at Rizhao Port was 708 RMB/ton. According to SMM survey data, as of July 16, the total inventory across the 10 ports monitored by SMM stood at 106.27 million tonnes, down 1.87 million tonnes from the previous period. Inventories of fines, concentrates, and pellets declined, while lump ore saw a slight accumulation. This indicates that overall iron ore demand remains resilient. Although market chatter suggests that environmental protection production restrictions have begun in Tangshan, they have not yet affected hot metal output. The iron ore fundamentals remain relatively stable. However, given expectations of reduced supply of Ukrainian concentrates due to the Russia-Ukraine conflict, and with the upcoming July Politburo meeting fueling strong market expectations for policy support in the second half of the year, a short-term price rebound is anticipated.
