## Main
in Chinese iron ore and steelmaking prices 12/01/2026
Dalian iron ore futures trended stronger today amidst vola lity. The benchmark I2605 contract ul mately closedat 822.5 RMB/tonne, represen ng an increase of 0.92% compared to the previous trading day. In the physical market, prices rose by 6-9 RMB/tonne. Traders were primarily focused on offloading cargoes at suitable price levels, while steel mills largely maintained a wait-and-see a tude, resul ng in a generally average trading atmosphere. According to the latest SMM sta s cs, global iron ore shipments for the current period totalled 31.88 million tonnes, a significant week-on-week decline of 5.98 million tonnes (a drop of 15.8%). Conversely, arrivals at major Chinese ports bucked this trend, rising slightly by 0.47 million tonnes (1.7%) to reach 27.95 million tonnes. This indicates that, in the short term, supply pressure remains persistent. Looking ahead, as the market formally enters the cri cal window for pre-Lunar New Year raw material restocking, rigid replenishment demand is expected to provide robust support for the price floor. However, given that port inventories remain at elevated levels, ample spot supply may cap the upside poten al for iron ore prices. Weighing the current interplay between supply and demand, iron ore prices are projected to con nue their pa ern of fluctua on at high levels in the near term.
