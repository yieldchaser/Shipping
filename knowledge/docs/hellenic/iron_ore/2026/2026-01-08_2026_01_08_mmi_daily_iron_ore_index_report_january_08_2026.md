## Main
in Chinese iron ore and steelmaking prices 08/01/2026
Dalian iron ore futures retreated from recent highs today, exhibiting a weak and volatile trend. The benchmarkI2605 contract closed at 813 RMB/ton, representing a decline of 0.37% from the previous trading session. In the physical market, prices dropped by 8-15 RMB/ton. Traders showed average enthusiasm for quoting, largely following prevailing market trends, while steel mill procurement remained strictly on a needs-basis. Consequently, overall market turnover was subdued. On the supply and demand front, the intensity of blast furnace maintenance has eased slightly, leading to a gradual recovery in hot metal output; this is providing a degree of support for iron ore demand. According to SMM survey data, inventories across 10 major ports underwent slight destocking this week, with total stocks recorded at 114.64 million tons-a week-on-week decrease of 0.57 million tons. Regarding market sentiment, following the aggressive rally of the past few days, some speculative capital engaged in profit-taking. Furthermore, market rumors circulating regarding the permission to discharge Jimblebar Fines from ports contributed to the correction in futures prices. Despite bullish fundamental data, activity in the physical market has cooled, with insufficient momentum to chase further price increases. The market is currently characterized by strong fundamental support but pressure on the futures board. However, given that the window for pre-Lunar New Year restocking remains open and hot metal output is on an upward trajectory, support at lower price levels remains firm. In summary, iron ore prices are expected to fluctuate at elevated levels in the short term.
