Main
The iron ore futures market on the Dalian Exchange showed weak and volatile trading today. The main contract I2609finally closed at 780.5 yuan/ton, down 0.06% from the previous trading session. Port spot prices fell slightly by 1-5 yuan/ton compared to the previous day.

## Main
The iron ore futures market on the Dalian Exchange showed weak and volatile trading today. The main contract I2609finally closed at 780.5 yuan/ton, down 0.06% from the previous trading session. Port spot prices fell slightly by 1-5 yuan/ton compared to the previous day. Traders showed moderate enthusiasm for quoting prices; steel mills were cautious in inquiries, with most transactions concluded at low prices; overall trading sentiment was average. The transaction price for PB fines in the Shandong port was 750 yuan/ton. In the Caofeidian port, the transaction price for PB fines was 756 yuan/ton, and for Jinbuba fines it ranged from 725 to 730 yuan/ton. According to today's industry data, the total inventory of the five major steel products continued its destocking trend, with demand weakening yet still showing resilience. Meanwhile, steel mill profits remained stable, leaving room for a slight increase in hot metal output. Coupled with the geopolitical impacts of the US-Iran conflict and the Russia-Ukraine conflict, short-term cost support persists. It is expected that iron ore prices will have underlying support in the short term and will continue to fluctuate within a narrow range.
