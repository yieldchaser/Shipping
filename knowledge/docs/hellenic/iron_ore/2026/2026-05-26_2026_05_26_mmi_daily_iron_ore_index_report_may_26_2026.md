## Main
On May 26, 2026, the iron ore futures market showed a weak trend today, with the main contract I2609 closing at 781 yuan/ton,down 1.95% from the previous trading session. Port spot prices fell by 5-10 yuan/ton compared to the previous day. Traders showed moderate enthusiasm in quoting prices; steel mills mainly purchased for immediate needs; overall spot market transactions remained low so far. According to SMM statistics, blast furnace maintenance this week is estimated to affect iron production by 1.3075 million tons, a decrease of 68,400 tons week-on-week. Next week, the impact is expected to further decrease to 1.2049 million tons, a reduction of 102,600 tons week-on-week. The impact of maintenance has narrowed for two consecutive weeks, indicating a gradual recovery in hot metal output and a simultaneous increase in both iron ore consumption and procurement by blast furnaces. This modestly boosts iron ore demand. Supported by the marginal improvement in demand, downward room for ore prices is expected to be limited in the short term, with prices likely to fluctuate within a narrow range.
