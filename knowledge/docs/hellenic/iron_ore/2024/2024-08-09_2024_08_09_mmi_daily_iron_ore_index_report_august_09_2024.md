## Main
in Chinese iron ore and steelmaking prices 09/08/2024
DCE iron ore futures saw a significant rebound in the morning, but fell back again in the afternoon, ending the day with a strong oscillation.The most-traded contract I2501 finally closed at 741.5 yuan/mt, up 0.27% for the day. Traders followed the market trend to sell; steel mills had fewer inquiries as the weekend approached. Overall market transactions were average today. In Shandong, PB fines traded at 760-770 yuan/mt, up 5-15 yuan/mt from yesterday; in Tangshan, PB fines traded at 775-780 yuan/mt, up 5-15 yuan/mt from yesterday As of August 9, the total inventory at the 35 ports tracked by SMM was 146.68 million mt, down 610,000 mt MoM, up 30.32 million mt YoY. Port inventories have decreased for two consecutive weeks, mainly due to a recent decline in port unloading efficiency, leading to a significant reduction in arrivals. Currently, port inventories remain at a high level, putting pressure on ore prices. Additionally, considering that pig iron production may significantly decrease next week, the short-term fundamentals of iron ore remain weak, making it difficult for ore prices to find upward momentum.
