## Summary
Main
in Chinese iron ore and steelmaking prices 04/09/2024

Steel mills were cautiously observing the market, mainly engaging in small and frequent purchases. ## Main
in Chinese iron ore and steelmaking prices 04/09/2024
Today, iron ore futures continued to decline, with the most-traded contract I2501 falling below 700 points, ultimately closing at 689.5, down 3.09% for the day.Traders showed a low willingness to sell. Steel mills were cautiously observing the market, mainly engaging in small and frequent purchases. Today's market transactions were sluggish. In Shandong, the transaction price of PB fines was around 690-695 yuan/mt, down 10-15 yuan/mt from yesterday; in Tangshan, the transaction price of PB fines was around 705-710 yuan/mt, also down 10-15 yuan/mt from yesterday. Last night, the ISM manufacturing PMI data released by the US fell short of market expectations, further raising concerns about the US manufacturing sector. Coupled with China's August PMI data released over the weekend, this led to a significant drop in ferrous metals series futures prices. From the perspective of iron ore fundamentals, the number of blast furnaces resuming production increased this week, with a slight rise in blast furnace operating rates and pig iron production. Although the pace of steel mills resuming production was slow, pig iron production is expected to increase significantly next week, which may provide some support for iron ore prices.
