## Main
in Chinese iron ore and steelmaking prices 25/12/2025
Iron ore futures fluctuated rangebound today, with the most-traded contract I2605 closing at 778.5, up0.58% from the previous trading day. Traders showed moderate willingness to sell; steel mills purchased as needed. Market trading activity was subdued. In Shandong, transaction prices for PB fines were 785-792 yuan, basically stable compared to yesterday; in Hebei, transaction prices for PB fines were 803-810 yuan/mt, also basically stable. Today's data indicated a slight decline in production of the five major steel products, with total inventory continuing to decrease. End-use demand is currently in the off-season, and apparent consumption is expected to have further room to decline, contributing to an overall weak market sentiment. However, as hot metal production gradually rebounds and pre-Chinese New Year stockpiling expectations strengthen after entering January, supportive news may increase. Fundamentally, the supply-demand structure of iron ore remains stronger than that of finished steel, showing relative resilience within the industry chain. Overall, iron ore prices are expected to continue a sideways movement in the short term.
