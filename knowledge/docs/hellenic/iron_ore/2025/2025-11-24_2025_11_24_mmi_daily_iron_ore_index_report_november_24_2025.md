## Main
in Chinese iron ore and steelmaking prices 24/11/2025
Today, Dce experienced a strong, fluctuating upward trend, with the main contract I2601 closing at 790.5 yuan, a slight increaseof 0.44% compared to the previous trading day. Traders demonstrated moderate enthusiasm in ofloading goods, while steel mills adopted a cautious wait-and-see approach, showing limited willingness to procure. Overall, market trading activity remained subdued. In the Shandong region, the transaction price for PB fines ranged between 789 and 795 yuan, up by 0-2 yuan/ton from last Friday's closing price. In the Hebei region, the transaction price for PB fines ranged from 798 to 802 yuan/ton, an increase of 0-3 yuan/ton compared to last Friday's closing price. Last week, global iron ore shipments tracked by SMM totaled 35.9 million tons, reflecting a slight increase of 2.6 million tons from the previous week, representing a growth rate of approximately 7.8%. Meanwhile, SMM reported that China's iron ore arrivals amounted to 25.53 million tons, a decline of 3 million tons from the prior week, marking a decrease of about 10.5%. This drop was observed across coarse fines, refined fines, and lump ore. In the short term, iron ore supply pressure remains manageable. Coupled with the continuous postponement of blast furnace maintenance and persistently high hot metal production, the fundamental support remains relatively strong. Key factors to monitor include seasonal demand weakness in the industry, changes in apparent steel consumption, and the timing of inventory inflection points.
