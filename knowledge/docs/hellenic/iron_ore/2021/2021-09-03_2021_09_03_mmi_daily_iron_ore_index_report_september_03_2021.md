## Main
in Chinese iron ore and steelmaking prices 03/09/2021
As of September 3, iron ore inventories across 35 ports tracked by SMM totalled 122.55 million mt, up 1.09 million mt from a week ago and 15.26million mt from a year ago. Daily average deliveries from the 35 ports decreased by 24,000 mt on a weekly basis to 2.67 million mt last week. The sharp decline in imported ore prices and output cuts at many steel mills curbed purchase activities. Despite fewer arrivals, inventory at the two main ports in Shandong began to increase as the efficiency of port operations has picked up. In contrast, inventory at the two main ports in Tangshan continued to decline due to three consecutive weeks of decline in arrivals and stable purchases by steel mills. Port inventory is likely to rise further due to growing arrivals and steel mill output cuts. DCE iron ore futures began to rise slightly today, the spot iron ore quotations basically keptstable. Traders were active to sell while steel mills tended to buy on their demand. The total deal atmosphere was not good over yesterday. PBF at Shandong port dealt 1020-1030yuan/mt, slightly increase by 0-5yuan/mt; PBF at Tangshan port dealt 1080yuan/mt, slightly decrease by 0-5yuan/mt.
