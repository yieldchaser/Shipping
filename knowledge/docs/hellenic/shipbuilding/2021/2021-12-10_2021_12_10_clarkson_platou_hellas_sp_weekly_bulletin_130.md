NEW BUILDING
In tankers this week, Clients of Eastern Mediterranean Maritime are reported to have ordered two firm 115k dwt LR2's at Daehan, with delivery of the
first vessel expected in May 2023 and the second vessel within July 2023.
In dry bulk, it was reported that Nantong Xiangyu took orders for four 63.5k dwt Ultramaxes from Huaxia Financial Leasing, with delivery of the vessels
expected within 1H 2024. Zhoushan Hongtong Shipping ordered two firm 14k dwt bulk carrier's at Zhoushan Hetai, with both vessels to be delivered
with 2023. Lixin Engineering took order for a firm 22.5k dwt bulk carrier from Fuzhou Huaming Shipping, with the vessel slated for delivery in 2023.
In the gas carrier market, Hudong Zhonghua announced taking orders for three firm 174k CBM LNG Carriers from United Liquified Gas, with the
vessels all set to be delivered within 2024. Samsung announced taking three separate orders for four firm 174k CBM LNG Carriers, all from unknown
owners, one pair of Vessels for delivery within 4Q 2024 and 1Q 2025 and two singular deals, these for delivery within Jan 2025 and May 2025
respectively.
RECYCLING
Edgy!
The ship recyclers appear to be spooked by the weakening steel markets on the global front causing yet further negative sentiment and a further drop
in indications from the waterfront. This is causing confidence to diminish when they consider offering for any available tonnage.
The Indian price levels seem to be hit more than their counterparts in Bangladesh and Pakistan, however these latter destinations have also been
negatively affected by the depressed steel markets and Pakistan further by the Pakistani rupee devaluation. The interesting factor is that price levels
continue to fall weekly despite the being a lack of tonnage being proposed to the market.
