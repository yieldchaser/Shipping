NEW BUILDING
In tankers, Samsung announced taking order for seven firm 120k dwt Shuttle Tankers from an unknown owner. Blocks are set to be fabricated at
Samsung with delivery of vessels expected at Zvezda Shipyard throughout 2024, 2025, 2026 and 2027. Meanwhile - It was reported that Zhoushan
Yongcheng Shipping ordered a firm 3.5k dwt chemical tanker at Haidong, bringing the series to two, with delivery of the vessel expected within 2023.
In dry bulk this week, Beihai announced taking order for two firm 210k dwt Newcastlemax from Chinese Maritime Transport, bringing their series to
four overall, with the vessels both set to be delivered by 3Q 2024.
In the gas carrier market, Daewoo (DSME) announced taking order for one firm 174k CBM LNG Carrier from an unknown owner, with delivery of the
vessel expected within 3Q 2024.
In containers, Hyundai Samho announced contracting two firm LNG dual-fuel 15,000 TEU containerships from Cido Shipping, with delivery of the
vessels set for 1Q and 2Q 2024 respectively.
RECYCLING
Pre-Diwali Boom!
Despite the quiet feel around the market, expectations are that we may about to witness a sudden influx of wet tonnage as price levels, particularly
from India, are exploding into life once again. Usually, pre-Diwali (Diwali - 4th November) does see excitable recyclers who are keen to fix tonnage at
this time of year, but we have seen an increase of about USD 30/ldt from the Indian market which may just bring more wet tonnage into the market.
The Indian buyers have completely washed away their counterparts from Pakistan and Bangladesh and are now offering the larger numbers from this
area, well in excess of the USD 600/ldt level, as evidenced by the sales collated below.
The domestic finished products prices in India have risen over the last week aiding this sudden improvement from the recyclers, the question is will
this just be a temporary increase or will the new levels continue ‘post-festivities'? News surfaced this week of China potentially cutting production and
rising demand across the globe. If this does occur, then we can certainly expect to see ship recycling prices continue in its current strong force for
some time.
