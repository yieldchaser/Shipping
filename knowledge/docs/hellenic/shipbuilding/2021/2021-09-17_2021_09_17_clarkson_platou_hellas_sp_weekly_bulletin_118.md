NEW BUILDING
In tankers this week, Vitol announced ordering two firm battery-hybrid fitted 8k dwt bunkering vessels at an unknown Chinese shipyard, with delivery
set for 2H 2022 and 1H 2023.
In dry cargo, Chengxi Shipyard announced contracts for three firm 88k dwt Kamsarmaxes with Ciner Denizcilik, with delivery of all three vessels
expected within 2H 2024.
In the gas carrier market, MOL announced contracting four firm 174k CBM LNG Carriers at Daewoo (DSME), with delivery of the first vessel in 1H
2024 and the final vessel within October 2024.
In the container market, Samsung announced signing six firm LNG Dual-Fuel 7,000 TEU boxships for an unknown European owner, with vessels set
to be delivered from the third quarter of 2023 and then throughout 2024. It was reported that Mawei Shipbuilding contracted two firm plus two optional
gearless 2,900 TEU vessels with TS Lines, with delivery of the firm vessels set for 2H 2023.
In the ferry market, Naikai Zosen announced taking order for a 600 passenger ferry from Taiwan Navigation, with delivery expected in August 2023.
RECYCLING
North or Southwards?
Iron ore prices are in a steep decline on China steel curbs as the Government attempt to slow property markets with a strong emphasis on controlling
pollution in general, but also with an eye on the Winter Olympics commencing in January 2022. This therefore could potentially dampen sentiment for
the recycling industry due to the concern of local demand for steel and subsequently, the local steel prices. In addition, a high volume of larger LDT
tanker units are again being reported sold into the Sub. continental markets and thus there is a hint that some negative sentiment may occur shortly.
For the time being, Pakistan is now at the forefront of their competitors and certainly appear willing to outbid their counterparts for any available larger
tanker unit. India looks set to seek tonnage requesting HKC Green recycling only and unwilling to compete for tonnage that are not of the ‘Green'
credentials. With almost all the operational recycling yards in Alang being HKC (Hong Kong Convention) compliant, much credit and praise must be
heaped on the local recyclers for an incredible achievement, allowing Owners the potential of reselling their aging units to conformable ship recycling
yards in the Indian sub. Continent.
Bangladeshi recyclers still have demand on the back of increasing infrastructure projects. This therefore should help indications to remain competitive
for the foreseeable future.
