NEW BUILDING
In tankers, Hyundai HI announced taking an order for four firm LNG dual-fuel 114k dwt LR2's from an unknown owner, with deliveries expected to run
from 3Q 2023 through to 2Q 2024.
In the gas carrier market, Samsung announced taking an order for four firm 174k CBM LNG Carriers from an unknown owner, with deliveries of the
vessels set for 2H 2024.
Meanwhile, in containers, it has been reported that Namsung Shipping ordered two firm 2,500 TEU Containerships at HMD, with both vessels set to
be delivered within 4Q 2023.
In other sectors, Taizhou Taichuan announced taking orders for two firm plus two optional 5,400 dwt MPP's from Guangxi Xinyi Supply Chain
Management, with delivery of the firm vessels expected in 2023. Nantong Rainbow Offshore & Engineering announced that an unknown owner has
ordered a firm 18.5k dwt Deck Cargo Carrier, with delivery expected in 2023.
RECYCLING
High Expectations!
With the Diwali festivities set for next week, the lack of tonnage is not helping the Indian recyclers to come forward with explosive offers and therefore,
unfortunately, we look to enter next week on a very low-key sentiment.
There is a lot of demand for steel in the Indian sub. Continent with many infrastructure projects in the pipeline and with steel at present not freely
accessible, price levels should remain firm for the time being. The dry freight rates have shown some weaker signs, however certainly not sufficient to
see some of these units enter the recycling market, therefore we will only continue to see tonnage proposed from the tanker, miscellaneous and
offshore sectors for the time being.
