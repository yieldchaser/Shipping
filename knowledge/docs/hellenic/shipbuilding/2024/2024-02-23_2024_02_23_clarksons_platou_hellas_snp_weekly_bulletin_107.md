New Building
In Tankers, Hanwha Ocean announced an order for a pair of conventionally fuelled 300K dwt VLCCs with an unknown owner, understood
to be clients of Evalend, with both vessels due to be delivered in 2H 2026. Albeit signed just before the Lunar New Year holidays, Dalian
Shipbuilding (DSIC) signed 6 + 2 x 300K dwt conventionally fuelled VLCCs with Seatankers delivering from 2H 2026 and through 2027. On the
smaller end of the spectrum, Seacon Shipping announced they have placed an order for 4 x 18,500dwt Chemical Tankers at Fujian Southeast,
China. These vessels will deliver in 2H 2025 and 1H 2026 respectively.
In LPG, Hyundai HI have announced an order for a pair of VLACs with an unknown owner. These 88K cbm units are reported to have been
placed by clients of Evalend and will deliver by the end of Feb 2027. Meanwhile, Hyundai Mipo have announced an order for a pair of 23K
LPG DF Fully Ref LPG Carriers. These are reported to have been signed with Pertamina and will deliver in 2Q & 3Q 2026.
Finally in containers, CMB.Tech have announced an order for a single 1,400 TEU Ammonia DF container ship for delivery from Qingdao
Yangfan for delivery in Mid 2026.
Finally, in the PCTC space, CMJL Nanjing have announced an order for a further pair of 7,000 CEU PCTCs with EPS. These latest ships are the
11th and 12th in the series and will deliver in 2H 2026. Neptune Lines meanwhile have themselves announced an order for 2 further 4,000 CEU
PCTCs, these their 3rd and 4th in the series contracted at Fujian Mawei. These latest ships due to deliver in 2027.

Recycling
Making Hay!
We have seen a slight upturn in price indications this week, most likely due to the dwindling supply of candidates. Reports suggest from
local sources that only one or two new vessels have arrived at the official anchorage for recycling clearance in Alang, and no new
units have arrived at Pakistan or Bangladesh. This certainly confirms the views that ship owners are making hay on their tonnage whilst
the sun shines, the sun being the improving freight/charter rates and ever-increasing second-hand values, even on older units, due to
the ongoing global dynamics.
