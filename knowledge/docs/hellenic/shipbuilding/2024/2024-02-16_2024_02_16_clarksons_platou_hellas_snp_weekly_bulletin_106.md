New Building
In tankers this week, Magni Partners declared two optional 320k dwt VLCC's at New Times, bringing their series on order to four at the yard.
These latest vessels will be fitted with LNG dual fuel propulsion and will deliver in 2027. COSCO Zhoushan announced contracting three firm
plus one optional 154k dwt Shuttle tankers with Knutsen NYK Offshore tankers, with the first firm vessel expected to deliver in 2026 and the
second and third firm vessel in 2027. Furetank announced orders for two firm 18k dwt Chemical Tankers at CMJL Yangzhou, with the vessels
set to deliver in 4Q 2026 and 1Q 2027 respectively. The vessels will have LNG dual fuel propulsion and brings their series to 21 at the yard.
In dry bulk, clients of Star Bulk Carriers announced contracting three firm 82k dwt Kamsarmaxes at Qingdao Yangfan, with the vessels set to
deliver in September 2025, April 2026 and June 2026.
In containers, clients of Danaos announced contracting two firm 8,258 TEU Containerships at Yangzijiang. The vessels will hold a methanol
ready class notation and are expected to deliver in 4Q 2026 and 1Q 2027.
Finally, in the cruise sector, Carnival Cruise Lines announced ordering one firm 5,374 berths, 6,600 passenger Cruise Ship at Meyer Werft. The
vessel will have LNG dual fuel propulsion and is slated for delivery in 2Q 2027.

Recycling
Apathetic!
There certainly appears to be a lack of activity and little motivation as the lacklustre market continues.
India remains on the sidelines with further reduction in rates being reported this week, however both recyclers in Bangladesh and
Pakistan look to be pushing hard to come out of their previous doldrums and actively look to acquire tonnage, however the lack of
available units is not satisfying their demands. Pakistan still faces uncertain reaction to the recent election results which may hold the
recyclers back from improving their rates for the time being.
There are still financial restrictions in both locations, although reports suggest these are slowly easing. However, any Letter of credit
required to be opened for tonnage over 10,000 LDT may still be limited to a handful of recyclers and could still be problematic in the
issuance of such large funding by the banks.
Moving West, Turkish recyclers continue to offer aggressive numbers with their positive indications maintaining and closing in on the
levels from their counterparts from India, but hereto, supply remains low.
