## Summary
Main
Nearing the end of the second week after the interim peace deal between Iran and United States began to break down, and amid renewed attackson vessels in the MEG, we've now reached the lowest daily total tonnage transit number through the SoH at 0.2m GT on Monday, amounting to >95% below typical daily volumes. Following the temporary easing of sanctions, Iran increased crude exports during the brief truce, leading to a rise in floating oil supplies. However, crude sales have remained subdued as China's independent refiners have opted for cheaper cargo from Iraq, the UAE, and Qatar.

## Main
Nearing the end of the second week after the interim peace deal between Iran and United States began to break down, and amid renewed attackson vessels in the MEG, we've now reached the lowest daily total tonnage transit number through the SoH at 0.2m GT on Monday, amounting to >95% below typical daily volumes. Following the temporary easing of sanctions, Iran increased crude exports during the brief truce, leading to a rise in floating oil supplies. However, crude sales have remained subdued as China's independent refiners have opted for cheaper cargo from Iraq, the UAE, and Qatar.
At the same time, energy shipping markets remain at very elevated levels across all segments as reflected on S&P asset prices.
This week saw healthy ordering activity spread across multiple shipping segments, including Suezmax tankers, MR tankers, LNG carriers, and VLGCs. Meanwhile, benchmark newbuild prices in the tanker market have continued climbing on the back of robust demand for new vessels - VLCCs NB prices are now 2% higher since the start of the year, Suezmaxes are up 4%, and MRs have risen by 5%.
In the secondhand market, the dry bulk sector once again accounted for the majority of transaction activity this week, with prominent demand observed for geared tonnage ranging from larger Handysize up to Ultramax bulk carriers. For comparison purposes in ECO Ultramaxes, although not identically designed, BLUE AKIHABARA (ABT 61,630 DWT, 2014, NANTONG COSCO KHI (NACKS), CR:4X30T) achieved a price of excess USD 25m this week, when in May the two-year-younger sister-vessels HUAYANG ROSE and HUAYANG LILY (ABT 63,562 DWT, 2016, CHINA SHIPPING INDUSTRY (JIANGSU), CR:4X30) were understood to have been concluded at xs USD 25m each basis an enbloc sale.
Dry Cargo
In the Capesize sector this week, the HEROIC (ABT 182,060 DWT, 2010, ODENSE LINDO) was concluded at USD 32.8m, to European interests.
Meanwhile, the eco Ultramax BLUE AKIHABARA (ABT 61,630 DWT, 2014, NANTONG COSCO KHI (NACKS), CR:4X30T, Electronic M/E) after receiving offers in early July, has now been concluded at excess USD 25m, with Greek buyers reported behind the acquisition.
Elsewhere, in the Supramax sector, the IVS CRIMSON CREEK (ABT 57,945 DWT, 2014, SK TOYOHASHI SB, CR:4X30.5T, Electronic M/E) also invited offers this week and we understand has seen excess USD 23.5 million.
Tanker
Although we understand several negotiations to be ongoing, there are no new tanker sales to report this week.
