Clarkson Hellas Ltd.
Desk Talk
This week, the Dry Bulk sector has been at the forefront of market attention. The already firm sentiment
strengthened further, supported by improving freight fundamentals, as reflected in the overall week-
on-week increase of the BDI. Numerous enquiries were recorded across all vessel types, with particular
focus on 15-20-year-old Panamax and Kamsarmax tonnage built in Japan, while demand for eco
bulkers remains firm amid limited availability of prompt candidates. Activity in the tanker segment has
maintained a more subdued tone, although we are aware of a fair number of ongoing negotiations.
Dry Cargo
In the Kamsarmax sector, the AVALON (ABT 81K DWT, 2011, SUNGDONG) is understood committed at
region USD 18m, whilst the older MANDY MORN (ABT 82K DWT, 2008, TSUNEISHI ZOUSHAN) was reported
sold to Chinese interests at levels circa USD 14m.
On the Panamax segment, the PRABHU YUVIKA (ABT 76K DWT, 2004, SUMITOMO) was sold at mid-high
USD 9m.
Market rumors indicate that a 2017 Chinese Ultramax is being negotiated in region USD 26/27m.
Moving to the Supramax size, the VALIANT WAVE (ABT 53K DWT, 2005, IMABARI) was committed at USD
10.5m. After inviting offers this week, we understand the SUMAQ QUEEN (ABT 51K DWT, 2017, IMABARI)
has seen interest from more than 5 buyers at mid USD 25m levels and is currently under negotiations.
We are hearing as well of a 2008 Mitsui sold for USD 14m basis very prompt delivery.
Lastly, the Handysize INTERLINK FORTUITY (ABT 38K DWT, 2017, TAIZHOU KOUAN, ICE CLASS 1C) was
acquired by German interests for USD 21.5m.
Tanker
On the Tanker side, although we are noticing various negotiations developing mainly across 15 to 20
year-old tonnage, there are no new tanker sales that we can report.
