## Main
Happy New Year!
Following the start of the year, we see continuous activity on both dry and tanker markets.
The dry cargo market has held up with a steady flow of transactions, while the tanker sector has seen particularly strong momentum, driven by significant VLCC deals that have set a confident tone for the opening weeks of the year.
Dry Cargo
In the larger segment, we are hearing rumors of a 2011 Japanese built Capesize being committed at levels of USD 32.5 m net, while at the Kamsarmax front, the CENTURY SHANGHAI (ABT 81K DWT, 2018, CHENGXI) has been sold via auction for USD 25.02 m.
In the Ultramax segment, the STARRY NIGHT (ABT 61K DWT, 2022, NANTONG COSCO) achieved a price of USD 32.5 m, basis March - April delivery.
Furthermore, another auction sale has been concluded, with the Supramax JIANG YUAN NAN JING (ABT 50K DWT, 2003, NACKS) achieving levels of USD 7.54 m.
In the Handysize sector, the FETHIYE-M (ABT 37K DWT, 2011, SPP GOSEONG) has now been sold to Greek interests in the high USD 12 m, while the BULKER BEE 30 (ABT 34K DWT, 2010, TK) has been sold at USD 11.3 m. Finally, we are hearing of a 2016 Tsuneishi Cebu built Handysize being committed for levels of USD 19.1 m.
Tanker
The key transaction of the week involved c/o Frontline selling 8 x VLCCs (2015-2016 built - 1st generation ECO m/e) for an aggregate value of USD 831.5 m, with deliveries scheduled during Q1 2026. In parallel, the owners have agreed to acquire 9 x Newbuilding VLCCs with scrubbers for the amount of USD 1.224 bn, with deliveries running from Q3 2026 throughout Q2 2027.
In addition, c/o CMB.TECH have sold 6 x VLCCs (2007-2016 built) enbloc, with deliveries also scheduled for Q1 2026, generating an estimated capital gain of around USD 261 m.

Desk Talk
Happy New Year!
Following the start of the year, we see continuous activity on both dry and tanker markets.
The dry cargo market has held up with a steady flow of transactions, while the tanker sector has seen
particularly strong momentum, driven by significant VLCC deals that have set a confident tone for the
opening weeks of the year.
Dry Cargo
In the larger segment, we are hearing rumors of a 2011 Japanese built Capesize being committed at
levels of USD 32.5 m net, while at the Kamsarmax front, the CENTURY SHANGHAI (ABT 81K DWT, 2018,
CHENGXI) has been sold via auction for USD 25.02 m.
In the Ultramax segment, the STARRY NIGHT (ABT 61K DWT, 2022, NANTONG COSCO) achieved a price
of USD 32.5 m, basis March - April delivery.
Furthermore, another auction sale has been concluded, with the Supramax JIANG YUAN NAN JING
(ABT 50K DWT, 2003, NACKS) achieving levels of USD 7.54 m.
In the Handysize sector, the FETHIYE-M (ABT 37K DWT, 2011, SPP GOSEONG) has now been sold to Greek
interests in the high USD 12 m, while the BULKER BEE 30 (ABT 34K DWT, 2010, TK) has been sold at USD 11.3
m. Finally, we are hearing of a 2016 Tsuneishi Cebu built Handysize being committed for levels of USD
19.1 m.

T anker
The key transaction of the week involved c/o Frontline selling 8 x VLCCs (2015-2016 built - 1st generation
ECO m/e) for an aggregate value of USD 831.5 m, with deliveries scheduled during Q1 2026.
In parallel, the owners have agreed to acquire 9 x Newbuilding VLCCs with scrubbers for the amount
of USD 1.224 bn, with deliveries running from Q3 2026 throughout Q2 2027.
In addition, c/o CMB.TECH have sold 6 x VLCCs (2007-2016 built) enbloc, with deliveries also scheduled
for Q1 2026, generating an estimated capital gain of around USD 261 m.
