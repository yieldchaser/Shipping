## Main
Both dry bulk and tanker secondhand markets have remained active, with bulker deals particularly active.
Transactions continue to materialize, reflecting sustained buyers' appetite despite asset prices across both sectors continuing to push at higher levels, supporting overall market confidence.
Dry Cargo
On the Kamsarmax front, c/o Agricore Shipping have concluded the sale of the ASL MOON (ABT 81K DWT, 2008, MITSUI TAMANO) at USD 13.6 m to Chinese interests.
Meanwhile, after having invited offers earlier in the week, the THREE SASKIAS (ABT 80K DWT, 2014, JMU TSU) has been committed at levels of USD 26.7 m to Far Eastern interests.
In addition, the GIEWONT (ABT 79K DWT, 2010, NEW CENTURY) has been concluded at USD 11.8 m, with Chinese being rumored as the buyers.
In the Supramax segment, c/o Densay Shipping have sold the SSI ERDOGAN BEY (ABT 50K DWT, 2010, OSHIMA) at USD 15 m, basis surveys freshly passed.
Finally, in the Handysize sector, the ADVENTURE (ABT 33K DWT, 2011, WEIHAI SAMJIN) has changed hands for USD 9.5 m basis a very prompt delivery with surveys imminently due.
Tanker
On the wet side, prices have continued to strengthen, with the present week forming no exception.
The scrubber fitted Suezmax SIGRUN (ABT 156K DWT, 2013, SUMITOMO) is reported sold at USD 65 m to C/O Naftomar.
Additionally, In the MR space, the scrubber-fitted sister vessels NORD MAVERICK and NORD MARVEL (ABT 50K DWT, 2020, HMD) have been committed on enbloc basis at levels in excess of USD 90 m to undisclosed buyers.
