## Main
Asset prices, particularly in the Tanker sector, have seen a remarkable upswing following the sharp spike in freight rates triggered by the developments in the Middle East.
We are also noticing strong competition on the Dry side, mainly for Japanese built tonnage, with some vessels being offered for sale managing to attract more than a dozen buyers.
Dry Bulk
The Capesize CAPE SANDRA (ABT 175K DWT, 2011, HHIC-SUBIC) has been reported sold at region USD 32 m, basis surveys due in May 2026.
On the Panamax front, c/o Evalend have concluded the sale of the sister-vessels TRINITY I & ROZA (ABT 93K DWT, 2010, JIANGSU NEW YANGZIJIANG) for an enbloc price in the region of USD 23 m.
Meanwhile, in the Handysize sector, after inviting bids last week, Japanese owners have now concluded the sales of the NORD SANTIAGO (ABT 39K DWT, 2018, JIANGMEN NANYANG) achieving USD 22 m, and the one year older ACTION TRADER (ABT 39K DWT, 2017, JIANGMEN NANYANG) for USD 20.5 m.
Further activity was recorded in the Handy segment, with the MANGUSTA (ABT 33K DWT, 2015, SHIN KURUSHIMA) achieving circa USD 16.5 m, and the KEN GIANT (ABT 28K DWT, 2009, I-S) being sold at USD 8.6 m to Turkish based buyers.
Tanker
The Aframax GREEN ATTITUDE (ABT 112K DWT, 2018, COSCO ZHOUSHAN) was sold at region USD 70 m to Greek interests, whilst the older Aframax SINBAD (ABT 116K DWT, 2009, SAMSUNG HI) was committed at region USD 40 m.
C/o Scorpio Tankers have sold their scrubber-fitted LR2 STI SOLIDARITY (ABT 110K DWT, 2015, SUNGDONG) for USD 60 m, and their 2x scrubber-fitted MR2 STI SENECA & STI OSCEOLA (ABT 50K DWT, 2015, HYUNDAI MIPO) for USD 35 m each.
