New Building
In tankers, New Times SB contracted four firm 320k dwt VLCC's with clients of Dynacom Tankers, with the vessels set to
deliver in 2H 2026 and 1H 2027. In dry bulk, Hengli Hi announced contracting ten firm 82k dwt Kamsarmaxes with clients of
Sea Traders, with the vessels set to deliver in from 2025 and throughout 2026. Chengxi Shipyard announced ordering two
firm 82,600k dwt Kamsarmaxes with Huaxia Financial Leasing, with the vessels to deliver in 2026. In containers, Huanghai SB
contracted four firm geared 850 TEU MPP Heavy Lift containerships with Nordic Hamburg, with the vessels set to deliver in
2025 and 2026. Briese Schiffarts also contracted two firm plus two optional geared 850 TEU MPP Heavy Lift containerships at
Huanghi SB, which are slated for delivery in 2025 and 2026.

Recycling
Lethargic!
Market conditions are rather sluggish this week where few, if any, transactions are being negotiated, creating a sluggish
vibe within the recycling community. The slow feel for the year is highlighted by the fact that not a single
VLCC/Suezmax/Aframax conventional tanker unit has been sold for recycling in the first 7 months of this year, a feat not
seen since 1970. This is attributed to the ever-growing ‘dark' tanker fleet, which has sucked all candidates away from
the recycling market over the past two years. Some negative sentiment seemed to echo across the Indian sub.
Continent recycling destinations this week with all areas expressing a downward trend in their respective price
indications. This is primarily on the back of the monsoon onslaught currently being experienced and weakened domestic
steel markets. Interesting reports arrived in the media this week with news that Petrobras has committed one of their
FPSO's internally to a Brazilian green recycling yard. Reports suggest that local recyclers have converted a local shipyard
to cater for Green ship recycling and the ‘Petrobras 32', about 37,300 ldt, built 1974 has been sold to the yard and whilst
it is the first recycling project of its kind in this destination, and obviously this decision is welcomed by the NGO
Shipbreaking Platform, time will tell if this yard will be able to decommission many units in a short space of time, or whether
capacity will be limited as evidenced with alternative EU approved recycling yards. We probably know the answer to
this despite the NGO claims of sufficient capacity!
