New Building
In tankers this week, James Fisher signed two firm, plus two optional LNG dual-fuel 6k dwt, IMO II, Chemical Tankers at CMJL
Yangzhou, with the firm vessels set to deliver in 2026.
A lot of activity in the gas carrier market this week, with Hyundai HI announcing contracts for seventeen 174k CBM LNG
Carriers for Qatar Energy. The vessels will deliver throughout 2027, 2028 with the final vessel set to deliver in September 2029.
CMHI (Jiangsu) announced contracts for two firm 180k CBM LNG Carriers with Celsius, with both vessels set to deliver in 2027.
In car carriers, Wallenius Wilhelmsen announced contracts for four firm 9,350 CEU PCTC's at CMJL Nanjing. The vessels will be
methanol dual-fuel and will deliver from July 2026 through to January 2027.
Finally, in MPPs, CSSC Guangxi announced contracting four firm, plus two optional 8.5k dwt General Cargo vessels with
Manisa Bulk, with the firm vessels expected to deliver in 2025.

Recycling
Alang Horror Show!
The Indian recycling market has experienced a ghostly role this week as a continual daily fall in the domestic steel prices has
filtered through to the recyclers, resulting in price indications from this destination reduce by some USD 20-30/ldt.
Local reports suggest that the weaker rates and uncertain domestic markets will continue to ensure the recyclers will remain
low-spirited until after their forthcoming Diwali festivities (12th November), the only blessing is that the lack of tonnage should
avoid any further dramatic reduction in price levels.
Elsewhere, the current ship recycling market in Bangladesh is struggling due to poor local demand. Weekly arrival reports at
the Chattogram anchorage clearly show the decreasing number of vessels arriving for recycling. Two main pitfalls affecting
this market are the lack of confidence in general business (upcoming national election) and the ongoing shortfall of currency
reserves which continues to cause issues opening Letters of Credit.
