New Building
In tankers this week, Wuhu Shipyard announced contracts for four firm 18.5k dwt Chemical Tankers with Union Maritime, with
the vessels expected to deliver within 2025. Wuhu also announced an order for EGPN for four firm 18.5k dwt Chemical tankers,
with the vessels set to deliver in 2025. It has been reported that SSCSSL ordered two firm 9.2k dwt chemical tankers at CSSC
Wuchang SB, with the vessels both set to deliver in 3Q 2024 and 1Q 2025 respectively.
In the gas carrier market, Samsung announced contracts for two firm 174k CBM LNG carriers for an unknown owner,
understood to be K Line, with the vessels expected to deliver in 3Q and 4Q 2026.
Recycling
Religious Break!
The market has remained inactive this week due to religious festivities and lack of tonnage supply. The Easter holidays,
together with the observing of Ramadan will ensure this lack of activity will continue for the next couple of weeks.
The lack of tonnage does not look set to change for some time as the freight rates on all sectors continue to rebound week
on week with fundamentals generally supportive towards these improvements.
We can also report some positive news concerning Bangladesh where this week, we delivered the LNG ‘Adriatic Energy', abt
32,000 ldt. This is the highest value deal delivered to this destination since October 2022 following the well-publicised financial
restrictions that Bangladesh has suffered and required a lot of effort, cooperation, and patience from both the Sellers and
Buyers to allow the smooth delivery. It does feel like Bangladesh is open for business once again!
We wish all those celebrating over the next two weeks, all the best for a happy and blessed Easter.
