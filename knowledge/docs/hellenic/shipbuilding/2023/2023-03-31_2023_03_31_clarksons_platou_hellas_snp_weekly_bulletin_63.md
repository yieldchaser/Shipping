New Building
In tankers this week, Hyundai Vinashin announced contracting one firm 115k LR2 product tanker with an unnamed owner,
with delivery expected in 1Q 2026. Hyundai Mipo announced taking orders for four firm 50k dwt MR's. We understand these
are with Nissen Kaiun, with delivery of the vessels throughout 2025, with the final vessel delivering in November. It has been
reported that Union Maritime ordered four firm plus four optional 18.5k dwt Chemical Tankers at Mawei Shipbuilding, with the
firm vessels slated for delivery within 2025. CSSC Wuchang announced orders for two firm 9.2k dwt Chemical tankers with SC
Shipping. The vessels will have stainless steel cargo tanks and are expected to deliver in 2025. Finally, Consort Bunkers signed
contracts for six firm 6.5k dwt Methanol Bunkering tankers at CMJL (Nanjing) with the vessels to deliver throughout 2025.
In the gas carrier market, Hyundai Samho announced orders for two firm 174k CBM LNG Carriers with an unnamed owner,
with the vessels to deliver in January and March of 2027 respectively. CSSC Jiangnan announced contracts for two firm 175k
CBM LNG Carriers with TPSH Leasing, to be chartered to Shandong Marine Energy, with both vessels set for 2026 delivery. It
has also been reported that Kawasaki HI contracted a 86.7k CBM VLGC with Kumai Navigation, with the vessel expected to
deliver in 2026.
Finally, in car carriers, CMHI (Jiangsu) took orders for two firm plus up to four optional 9,300 CEU PCTC's from China Merchants
Shipping. The vessels will be Methanol Capable, with the firm vessels set to deliver in 2H 2025.

Recycling
Dried Up!
The supply of tonnage to the market has slowed considerably with only a handful of units being circulated into the market.
The question is whether they will be sold for recycling or if some traders come out of the woodwork whilst charter rates
improve.
Clients of Evergreen this week circulated two sister container vessels, the ‘Ever Unific' and the ‘Ever Uberty', both built 1999
and of about 24,300 ldt. Offers are due to be registered by 7th April, 2023 and reports suggest that there are some potential
trading buyers showing interest so time will tell whether they find have a final voyage to a recycling yard.
There have been reports that the steel markets in Bangladesh have weakened this week, some parties suggesting the
equivalent of about USD 40-50/ldt but as yet, we are not seeing signs of such a drop.
With Ramadan now under way and the forthcoming global Easter holidays creeping up, this quiet and inactive period looks
set to continue for several weeks.
