New Building
In tankers this week, it has been reported that Clients of Cardiff ordered three firm 115k dwt LR2 Product Tankers at COSCO
HI (Yangzhou), with deliveries expected in June, September and December 2025. The vessels will be scrubber fitted. Erik Thun
AB announced ordering four firm 8k dwt Chemical tankers at Ferus Smit SY, with the first vessel to be delivered in 2024 and
the remaining vessels in 2025.
In dry bulk, COSCO HI (Zhoushan) announced contracting four firm 63.6k dwt Ultramaxes for an unknown European owner,
with the vessels to deliver in February, April, June and August 2025. It has been reported that Fujian Guohang Group ordered
four firm plus two optional 73.8k dwt Panamax bulkers at Haitong Offshore, with the first firm vessel to deliver in 4Q 2024 and
the remaining firm vessels in 2025. Erik Thun also announced ordering four firm 5.1k dwt mini-bulker at Ferus Smit SY, with the
first firm vessel to deliver in 4Q 2024 and the remaining firm vessels in 2025.
In the gas carrier market, Hyundai HI announced contracting three firm 200k CBM LNG Carriers for an unknown owner,
reported to be Clients of Dynagas, with the vessels slated for delivery in May, August and November 2026. Hyundai Samho
announced contracting two firm 174k CBM LNG Carriers for an unknown owner, with the vessels expected to deliver in August
and November 2026. Hyundai HI also announced contracting a pair of LPG DF 88k CBM VLGC's for undisclosed owner, with
the vessels set to deliver in November 2025 and February 2026.

Recycling
Year of the Rabbit!
As we hop towards the end of January, market activity remains distinctively quiet with the lack of available tonnage still
evident and only a smattering of units seemingly workable.
Some concerning news emanated from Pakistan this week where the Pakistan Ship Breakers Association made a direct
appeal to their Prime Minister and government to save their industry from complete closure.
The recent financial turmoil that has engulfed the country has resulted in the reluctance of banks to open Letters of Credit
(L/C's) to import vessels for recycling which in turn affect the production of raw materials for the steel industry. The delay, or
non-opening, of L/C's for ship recycling will have a negative effect on the ship recycling industry and subsequently the steel
and construction sectors. The direct appeal from the association this week is reportedly a final push to obtain some assistance
and help in saving all industries connected with ship recycling including construction and the domestic steel markets.
Elsewhere, Bangladesh continues to suffer from a similar situation to Pakistan with only the smaller units able to deliver to the
recycling yards and therefore, India continues to be the most dominant with the vibrant domestic steel markets providing
firm price levels for any vessel that is circulated.
With the Lunar holidays commencing from today, we are expecting a very quiet time in shipping as a whole next week.
