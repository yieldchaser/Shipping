New Building
In dry bulk this week, New Dayang announced contracts for four firm 82k dwt Kamsarmaxes with an unnamed Hong Kong
owner, reported to be Cido, with the vessels delivering within 2026. Clients of Globus Maritime announced ordering two firm 64k
dwt Ultramaxes at an unnamed Japanese Shipyard, understood to be Imabari. The vessels will both deliver in 2H 2026.
In the gas carrier market, CSSC Dalian Shipbuilding announced contracts for two firm plus two optional 175k CBM LNG Carriers
with Sea Jade Investment (Joint venture between Wah Kwong, CSSC and China Gas), with the firm vessels set to deliver in
2027. Hyundai HI announced contracting two 88k CBM VLGC's for an unnamed owner. These are widely reported to be
Solvang's two optional vessels which brings their series to five at the yard, with the vessels expected to deliver in 2Q 2027 and
3Q 2027 respectively. Avance Gas announced declaring their two optional 40k CBM MGCs at CIMC SOE. The vessels will be
LPG dual-fuel and will deliver in 2Q 2026 and 4Q 2026, bringing their series to four at the yard.

Recycling
One small step, but no giant leap!
As India celebrates the ‘Chandrayaan-3' mission to the Moon, making space history being the first nation to land at the Moon's
South Pole, no such celebrations are paramount from the Alang recycling yards with local market sentiment remaining
cautious. There is currently no serious demand of the finished products from the domestic rolling mills, hence the weaker price
levels on offer.
As such, one small step from Pakistan is evident, but this is no giant leap. A number of recyclers are returning to the market with
reports of a few vessels being committed to this destination once again. However, some remain sceptical as to how the
process of delivery will occur and how long it will take for the Letters of Credit to be opened on arrival to the official anchorage
- time will tell in the forthcoming weeks if there are lengthy delays due to this financial situation. But whilst this is not a giant
leap, some optimism is apparent, hopeful that competition to their counterparts will once again be seen as the Gadani
destination is certainly required once the expected flow of tonnage gathers momentum.
We are certainly witnessing more dry bulk tonnage circulating into the market, clearly evidencing the pressure that the current
freight rates are under. With Bangladesh again facing financial issues, it is hoped that Pakistan can receive tonnage without
any delays or further financing issues to take the burden off the Indian recyclers.
