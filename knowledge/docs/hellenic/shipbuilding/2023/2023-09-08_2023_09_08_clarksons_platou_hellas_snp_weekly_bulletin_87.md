New Building
In dry bulk this week, CSSC Qingdao Beihai announced orders for two firm 325k dwt VLOC's from Winning International, with
the vessels set to deliver in 2026. The yard also announced orders for three firm plus three optional 210k dwt Newcastlemaxes
with EPS. The vessels will hold an "Ammonia Ready" class notation and are expected to deliver within 2026. It has been
reported that clients of Sea Traders ordered four firm 82k dwt Kamsarmaxes at Qingdao Yangfan, with the vessels set to deliver
in 2H 2026.
In tankers, Sirius Rederi announced ordering two firm plus two optional 15k dwt Chemical Tankers at CMJL Yangzhou. The
vessels will hold a "Methanol Ready" Class Notation, will be Battery Hybrid fitted and the firm vessels will deliver in 2026.
A busy week in the gas carrier market, as Hyundai HI announced two separate orders for two firm plus one optional LPG dual-
fuel 88k CBM VLAC's with clients of Capital and EPS. The firm vessels are all expected to deliver within 2H 2027. CSSC Jiangnan
announced contracts for two firm 93k CBM VLAC's with EPS. The vessels will deliver within 2027 and will bring the series to six
at the yard.
In car carriers, clients of Neptune Lines announced ordering two firm 4,200 CEU PCTC's at Mawei. The vessels will have LNG
dual-fuel propulsion and are set to deliver within 2026.
Finally, Damen Yichang announced contracting a 3.8k dwt, 172 TEU and a 5k dwt, 164 TEU MPP's with Feyz Group. The vessels
are both slated for delivery in 4Q 2024.

Recycling
Volatility speaks volumes!
There seems to be two mindsets from recyclers in Alang, India this week. Some recyclers appear to be increasing their
interest to procure tonnage and we have seen improved rates by some USD 15-20 per ldt this week. However, some with
inventories on their yards, are reportedly adopting a wait and see approach with a less than optimistic sentiment about
the future market conditions. Fundamentally, these improvements could be short-lived as ingot prices have increased,
but demand for finished products remains low.
The increased impetus from India may also be on the back of the renewed vigour from Pakistan as this market certainly
feels like they are once again ‘open for business'. Letters of Credit are being opened once again and more vessels seem
to be heading to this direction. With more units arriving to this destination, some predict this may be a temporary reprieve
as the finance may be saturated in the short term. But for now. Pakistan certainly looks like the ‘go-to' market for the
foreseeable future.
