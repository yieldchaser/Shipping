New Building
In dry bulk this week, it has been reported Clients of Sea Pioneer Shipping ordered three firm 82k dwt Kamsarmaxes at Oshima
Shipbuilding, with the first two vessels expected to deliver within 2Q 2025 and the final vessel in 3Q 2025.
In the gas carrier market, Hyundai Samho announced orders for two firm 88k CBM VLGC's for ABGC. The vessels are both
fitted with LPG dual fuel propulsion with the first vessel set to deliver in 4Q 2025 and the second vessel in January 2026. ABCG
also announced ordering a firm LPG dual-fuel 86.7k CBM VLGC at Kawasaki HI, with the vessel slated for delivery in 2026.
Finally, in the cruise sector, it has been reported that Sunstone Ships ordered a 200 Passenger Cruise vessel at CMHI (Jiangsu).
The vessel is expected to deliver in 2025, which brings their series to 7 at the yard.
Recycling
Thin!
Market supply remains thin on the ground, however we are certainly seeing more inquiry and facing more questions from ship
owners relating to the ship recycling industry. More supply looks set to come from the container feeder type sector, although
the actual deluge of such tonnage that parties are predicting will not materialise for many months yet in our opinion.
Some encouraging news from India arrived this week by way of assistance to the recycling industry by the Indian
Government. A notification was circulated announcing that all duties on importing tonnage for recycling which should help
to increase market sentiment/rates by some 3-5%.
Following many months of low activity due to the ongoing Letter of Credit issues, the recycling market in Bangladesh looks to
have finally resurrected with more Letters of Credit being opened, still for smaller units, but also possibly for larger financial
backing. With this in mind, price levels for the smaller units should improve pre-Ramadan. It is believed that the recyclers have
found alternative methods of finding ways to purchase tonnage. There will certainly be more optimism once we see more
supply arriving, and delivering, to the yards in Chattogram.
Pakistan unfortunately remains quiet.
