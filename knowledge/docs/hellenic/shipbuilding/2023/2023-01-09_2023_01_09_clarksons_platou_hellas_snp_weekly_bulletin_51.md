New Building
In dry bulk this week, it has been reported that Ocean Agencies ordered two firm 63k dwt Ultramaxes at Sumec New
Dayang, with the vessels set to deliver in May and June 2025 respectively.
In the gas carrier market, CMHI Jiangsu announced contracts for four firm 180k CBM LNG Carriers for an unknown owner,
understood to be Celsius, with the vessels expected to deliver in 2026.
In containers, it has been reported that Lepta ordered two firm plus two optional 8,000 TEU Containerships at Yangzijiang,
with the firm vessels expected to deliver in 2025.
In the cruise sector, CMHI Jiangsu announced taking order for a 200 passenger Polar Expedition Cruise Ship, with the vessel
set to deliver in 3Q 2025.
Recycling
Advantage Alang!
Today, we celebrate the feast of the Epiphany, however there is no party bearing gifts to the world of ship recycling, except
for perhaps India.
The well-publicised financial woes in Bangladesh have continued into the New Year and now, Pakistan has also had negative
news this week. The Pakistan Central Bank has announced that they will not open any fresh Letters of Credit for the local ship
recyclers and instead are concentrating on the financing of their internal humanitarian packages. Several vessels that were
being sold to Pakistan are now being diverted to Alang, India where there are no such financial difficulties.
The Indian market remains in a positive mode with all the domestic commodity markets supporting this upbeat mood. There
are reports that the Indian Government wish to compete with China as a manufacturing hub and attempts to modernise the
roads, rail networks and ports could result
in an increase in steel demand throughout 2023. Therefore, as the lack of available tonnage continues, price increases for
India may well be seen in the forthcoming weeks/months.
