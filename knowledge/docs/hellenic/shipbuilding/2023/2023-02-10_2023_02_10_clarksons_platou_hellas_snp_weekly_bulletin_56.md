There have been rumours circulating this week that the financial restrictions that have been imposed on the recyclers in
Bangladesh may be eased in March or April, 2023. The problems surrounding the issuance of letters of credit over the last
several months do appear to be improving, with more units reportedly being resold to the local recyclers, albeit the smaller
ldt size. However, some optimism has reverberated from the area this week, although there is still no genuine/official report
from the domestic banks to authenticate this, but a light could be around the corner.
With Pakistan almost non-existent, India remains the most reliable market at this current time with price levels being indicated
around the USD 600/ldt level for containers and back towards the mid 500's for dry bulk tonnage. Hunger and appetite remain
aggressive from these recyclers to procure tonnage whilst the steel demand locally outweighs the supply, although we are
certainly witnessing more of a steady stream into the marketplace.
Meantime, our thoughts go to all those in Turkey and Syria who have been tragically affected by the devastating earthquake
that occurred this week. Our sincere prayers are with all those that have suffered this week.
