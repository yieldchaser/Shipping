New Building
In tankers this week, clients of Atlas announced contracting two firm 156k dwt Suezmaxes at DH Shipbuilding. The vessels are both slated for
delivery in 2026 and bring their series to six at the yard. Clients of Aegean ordered two firm 114k dwt LR2s at COSCO Yangzhou. The vessels
will be Ice Class fitted and are both expected to deliver in 2H 2026. New Jiangzhou Shipping announced contracting two firm plus two
optional 13.8k dwt Chemical tankers for Alita, with the firm vessels set to deliver in 2025.
In the gas carrier market, Hyundai Samho announced contracts for four firm 93k CBM VLACs, understood to be for Maersk Tankers, with the
vessels expected to deliver from 1Q 2027 through to 4Q 2027. Hanwha Ocean announced contracting a firm 93k CBM VLAC with an
unnamed owner, with the vessel set to deliver in 3Q 2026. Hyundai Mipo disclosed orders for two firm 45k CBM MGCs for an unnamed owner,
reported to be Nissen Kaiun, with the vessels both set to deliver within 2H 2026. Shenzhen COSCO LPG Shipping ordered a firm 9k CBM
Ethylene / LPG carrier at COSCO Dalian, which will deliver in 1H 2026. They also ordered firm 6.3k CBM LPG Carrier at the yard, which is set to
deliver in 2H 2025.

Recycling
Regulatory advent?
The ‘possible positive' report circulating this week was the EU considering once again to put some selected yards in Alang (India) on to
the approved EU list of recycling facilities. This would be a huge step to help capacity for EU approved facilities as currently, we are
heavily relying on Turkey to absorb the larger tonnage that comes for sale requiring EU approval. The aim is that by 24th March, 2024
there will be some progression in talks, but many still believe this will take 1-2 years with efforts from the Indian Government and EU
organisations to provide some breakthrough. Whether the NGOs accept this cleanly remains to be seen but, in the meantime, we are
yet to see an official announcement by the EU.
Another potential piece of good news derives from Pakistan. An announcement has been made confirming its accession to the Hong
Kong Convention at the International Maritime Organization Assembly meeting this week and will shortly deposit the instrument of
ratification with the regulator. The Pakistan government had agreed to sign up to the convention after the 15 November cabinet
meeting. Before it enters into force, Pakistan's shipbreaking yards will have to upgrade facilities and operating practices at the main
shipbreaking site in Gadani to meet the standards of the Hong Kong Convention, a willingness they seemingly are prepared to do.
Moving to the current market conditions, India has seen daily domestic falls in their steel markets and no-one from the waterfront really
knows the reason why. They indicate the domestic lack of demand for steel but considering how the scrap market in the international
arena is currently playing, and the lack of tonnage arriving for dismantling, it shouldn't really be falling so the expectation, and hope,
is it may be a short-term repositioning.
