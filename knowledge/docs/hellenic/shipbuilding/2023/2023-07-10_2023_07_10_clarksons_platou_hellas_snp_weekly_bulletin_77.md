New Building
In Gas Carriers this week, an unnamed owner, reported to be NYK, ordered two firm 174k CBM LNG carriers at Hyundai Samho
and are set to deliver in 2Q 2027 and October 2027 respectively. Hyundai HI (Ulsan) contracted three firm 88k CBM VLGC's
for an unnamed owner. The vessels will deliver in 3Q and 4Q 2026 with the final to be delivered in December 2026. Sahara
group (West Africa LPG) have ordered two firm plus one optional 40k CBM MGC's at Hyundai Mipo, with delivery slated for
4Q 2025 and 1Q 2026. An unnamed owner, reported to be PascoGas, ordered a firm 45k CBM MGC at Hyundai Mipo, with
delivery set for October 2026.
In Car Carriers, Hyundai Mipo announced an order four firm 7,700 CEU PCTC's with an unnamed owner, reported to be Ray
Car Carriers, with the first two vessels expected to deliver in 2H 2026, and the final two vessels in 1H 2027.
Finally, in Containers, Clients of Danaos Shipping have ordered two firm conventionally fuelled, scrubber fitted, 8,000 TEU
Containerships at Yangzijiang, with both vessels expected to deliver in 2Q 2026.

Recycling
Return to the table?
Some interesting news circulated within the industry this week with reports that Pakistan may soon be in a position to
return to the bidding table.
The Pakistani rupee strengthened against the U. S. Dollar this week in their first trading session on the back of a funding
agreement from the International Monetary Fund (IMF). This could be significant for the recycling industry as will once
again create competition to their counterparts in Bangladesh and India, obviously for ‘non-green' units. The general
view is that there would be significant demand from the recyclers to procure tonnage following major internal disasters
and the potential rebuilding programmes that are sure to occur following this financial assistance.
Elsewhere, Bangladesh appears to be the most stable area in the Indian sub. Continent, although the ongoing Letter of
Credit issues still surface. Price levels from India this week softened, not only due to the current monsoon season which
seasonally creates negative sentiment, but also surrounds subdued local steel markets. Price levels are somewhat non-
competitive with their counterparts from Chattogram.
Moving West, the Turkish market is receiving several proposals for E.U. approved recycling which will once again raise
the capacity questions and may lead to some staggered deliveries for such business, and the expectation is to see some
more weakening of the Turkish Lira against the U.S. Dollar which may see market take a slight dip during the summer
months. But if the supply slows, then possibly improvements will be seen towards the end of the summer holiday season.
