New Building
In tankers this week, Euronav ordered one firm 319k dwt VLCC at Qingdao Beihai, with the vessel set to deliver in Q4 2026
bringing their series to three. Euronav also announced ordering two firm 157k dwt Suezmaxes at DH Shipbuilding. The vessels
are ice class fitted and the vessels are expected to deliver in April and May 2026. Dalian announced contracting two firm plus
two optional 115k dwt Aframaxes with an unnamed owner, reported to be clients of Arcadia, with the firm vessels slated for
delivery in 1H 2027 from their Shanhaiguan facility.
In dry bulk, a busy week with Qingdao Beihai announcing contracts for four firm methanol dual fuel 325k dwt VLOC's for
Shandong Shipping to be chartered to Vale. The vessels will be delivered in 2H 2026 and 1H 2027. Chengxi Shipyard
announced contracting four firm 82k dwt Kamsarmaxes for ICBC Leasing, with the vessels set to deliver in 2H 2026 and 1H 2027.
Wuhu Shipyuard announced contracting four firm plus six optional 89k dwt post panamax bulkers with Fujian Guohang Group.
The firm vessels are expected to deliver in 2H 2025 and 1H 2026.
In gas carriers, Jiangnan announced orders for three firm 99k CBM VLECs for Pacific Gas, with the vessels slated for delivery in
June, August and October 2027.
Recycling
Game Changer?
As we head deeper into December and towards the festive holidays, market activity remains limited with few ships being
made available for a recycling sale and, looking at the ‘arrivals' list at the recycling destinations in the Indian sub-Continent, it
is evident tonnage is sparse by the lack of new arrivals. Price levels remain stable as per recent weeks for the time being from
all recycling destinations.
Meantime, following on from last week's report where further ‘Green' credentials were reported from certain areas in an effort
to clean up the industry, there has been an announcement today where the United Arab Emirates (UAE) have introduced a
strict ship recycling regulation with their intention to deliver more robust and extreme responsible ship recycling program to
the ship recycling facilities in operation in the UAE.
The UAE Ship Recycling Regulation (UAE SRR) appears to be on the outside, more severe than the EU SRR as it bans not only
beaching, but also landing at an intertidal zone - the question is whether this will also apply to Turkey in addition to the Indian
sub-Continent, or will it apply purely to the few UAE recycling facilities.
From the initial reports perused through today, it would appear the new regulations, that will come into effect in June 2025,
shall apply to:
(a) UAE flagged vessels.
(b) Foreign tonnage recycled in the UAE.
(c) Foreign ships where the decision to recycle was made while the vessel was in EAE waters.
(d) Foreign tonnage performing final last voyage for recycling directly from UAE waters.
Item (d) above could be of particular concern as the strategy behind this could stop all ‘as is' deliveries to cash buyers (no
change of ownership) in UAE waters prior to ballasting the final voyage to the recycling destination. Further clarifications
should be known in the weeks ahead and what repercussions, if any will affect the global recycling destinations.
