## Summary
Main
Dry Cargo
With the Christmas holidays drawing near and the freight market easing, S&P activity has slowed down, as many prospective buyers are postponing their purchasing plans until the new year. Nonetheless, there remains a healthy buying appetite with main culprits still being Chinese buyers for the older tonnage, whilst enquiry for the younger, and especially eco type tonnage, coming from the West. The standout sale of the week was the Newcastlemax ATLANTIC LION (ABT 210K DWT, 2020, SWS, SCRUBBER), with surveys freshly passed, which was reported sold to Korean interests at USD 73.75 m.

## Main
Dry Cargo
With the Christmas holidays drawing near and the freight market easing, S&P activity has slowed down, as many prospective buyers are postponing their purchasing plans until the new year. Nonetheless, there remains a healthy buying appetite with main culprits still being Chinese buyers for the older tonnage, whilst enquiry for the younger, and especially eco type tonnage, coming from the West.
The standout sale of the week was the Newcastlemax ATLANTIC LION (ABT 210K DWT, 2020, SWS, SCRUBBER), with surveys freshly passed, which was reported sold to Korean interests at USD 73.75 m. The last comparable transaction is the enbloc sale of the 2 Newcastlemax vessels BULK SANTOS / BULK SYDNEY (ABT 208K DWT, 2020, NEW TIMES) at a total price of USD 145.5 m (USD 72.75 m each).
On Kamsarmaxes, we are hearing unconfirmed rumors of a 2021 Kamsarmax built at a Japanese-affiliated yard, being committed in the high USD 33's m levels. Whilst on the Ultramax segment, following last week's invitation for offers, Oceanstar Management have now secured the eco type IVS WINDSOR (ABT 60k DWT, 2016, OSHIMA) at a price in the low/mid USD 25 m range.
Tanker
Meanwhile, on the tanker front, all eyes are on the two VLCCs TWIN POLLUX / TWIN CASTOR (ABT 320K DWT, 2014/2013, BOHAI), which invited offers this week. The outcome of the process is expected to provide valuable insight into current appetite for modern large crude tonnage.
We expect clarity to emerge shortly and look forward to providing an update in next week's report.
