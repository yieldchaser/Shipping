## Main
Dry Cargo
S&P activity has remained steady and continued at the same pace this week, with a solid flowof transactions taking place and no immediate indications that such conditions are likely to shift any time soon. Demand for dry cargo bulkers and particularly high-quality units built at reputable shipyards, remains strong.
On the larger sizes, the Imabari built SEACON AFRICA (ABT 206K DWT, 2006, IMABARI) was sold at USD 22.7 m to Chinese buyers, whilst on Panamaxes, the ORNAK (ABT 79K DWT, 2010, NEW CENTURY) which invited offers on the 27th of Oct, has been sold achieving levels in the Low 11's m.
Most activity this week was centered around geared vessels, with the most notable sale in the Ultramaxes being the CMB BRUEGEL (ABT 63K DWT, 2021, SHIN KASADO) which has been sold to Meghna Group, Bangladesh for a price in the region of USD 33 m basis delivery in Q1 2026 with surveys freshly passed.
On Supramaxes, the INCE FORTUNE (ABT 57K DWT, 2010, STX DALIAN) has achieved a price in the Mid-to-High USD 12's m levels, whilst the KARADENIZ S (ABT 57K DWT, 2012, STX SB KOREA) is committed at levels slightly in excess of USD 14 m to Turkish interests.
On Handies, the YANGTZE FLOURISH (ABT 32K DWT, 2012, JIANGMEN NANYANG) has been sold for a price in the High USD 9's m to Chinese buyers.
Tanker
It has been a rather active week in terms of on-going discussions taking place in the products and crude sectors. Most notable sales are the 4x 2014 HMD built Scrubber fitted MR's (namely STI BATTERY, STI VENERE, STI MILWAUKEE, and STI YORKVILLE) that Scorpio Tankers has sold for region USD 128 m enbloc i.e. USD 32 m per vessel, basis delivery during Q1 2026.
At the same time, Scorpio Tankers announced it agreed to purchase 4x scrubber fitted MR Newbuildings, currently under construction at Jingjiang Nanyang Shipbuilding, China at a purchase price of USD 45 m per vessel and with expected deliveries 2Q-3Q 2026 and 1Q-2Q 2027.
