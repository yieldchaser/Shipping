## Summary
Main
Dry Cargo
As the year draws to a close, activity slowed during the first week of December in both the dry and the wet sectors. In the Kamsarmax segment, following last week's invitation for offers, the KEY FRONTIER (ABT 80K DWT, 2011, UNIVERSAL SB MAIZURU) has been acquired by Greek interest at USD 18.7 m. Meanwhile, the BW MATSUYAMA (ABT 81K DWT, 2019, TSUNEISHI CEBU) remains under negotiations.

## Main
Dry Cargo
As the year draws to a close, activity slowed during the first week of December in both the dry and the wet sectors.
In the Kamsarmax segment, following last week's invitation for offers, the KEY FRONTIER (ABT 80K DWT, 2011, UNIVERSAL SB MAIZURU) has been acquired by Greek interest at USD 18.7 m. Meanwhile, the BW MATSUYAMA (ABT 81K DWT, 2019, TSUNEISHI CEBU) remains under negotiations.
Within the Supramax sector, two additional sales were recorded. The JIN BI (ABT 56K DWT, 2012, JIANGSU NEW HANTONG, electronic main engine) was confirmed sold to Xing Le Investments, Hong Kong at USD 14.4 m, while the INCE KASTAMONU (ABT 56K DWT, 2010, COSCO ZHOUSHAN) changed hands to undisclosed buyers at USD 12.8 m. Overall, the Supramax market has shown firming sentiment in recent months, with asset values trending upward compared to the summer period, when a 2012 Chinese built 56K DWT vessel achieved a price of USD 13.25 m, while a 2010 Chinese built 58K DWT was sold at USD 11.2 m.
The Ultramax IVS WINDSOR (ABT 60K DWT, 2016, OSHIMA SB) invited offers this week and we are waiting to see the outcome, which we will be able to report further next week.
Tanker
Another relatively slower week on the tanker market, with no confirmed sales reported.
