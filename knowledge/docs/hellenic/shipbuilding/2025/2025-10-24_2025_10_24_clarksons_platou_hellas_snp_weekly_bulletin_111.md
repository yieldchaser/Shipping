## Main
Dry Cargo
Another very active week for Bulk carriers with numerous vessels changing handsand transactions taking place across all segments. Despite the small decline of the BDI since the start of the week, we do not interpret this as a signal for Buyers to pause their acquisition efforts. On the contrary, we expect purchasing activity to remain robust, as an increasing number of enquiries continue to emerge.
Starting with the Newcastlemax sector, following their recent sale of three units to UAE based buyers, Messrs ‘2020 Bulkers' have now offloaded another vessel from their modern fleet, namely the BULK SAO PAULO (ABT 208K DWT, 2020, NEW TIMES, SCRUBBER) which has achieved a price of USD 72.25m from Belgian Buyers. The Capesize segment remains very active as well, with the ROSEMARY (ABT 179K DWT, 2010 DSME), which was inviting offers on the 21st of October, getting tied up at levels of around USD 28.5m, while the one year younger CMB-owned MINERAL SUBIC (ABT 179K DWT, 2011 HANJIN SUBIC) has managed to achieve levels of low USD 30m from Chinese interests. On the older tonnage, Mssrs ‘Seacon' have emerged as the Buyers of a 2006 blt unit owned in China, the AP POWER (ABT 173K DWT, 2006 BOHAI), which has achieved levels of mid USD 14m.
Less activity has been taking place on the Kamsarmax sector, with the most notable sale of the week being the FJELD SAGA (ABT 83K DWT, 2013, SANOYAS) which changed hands for a price of circa USD 20m from Messrs ‘ADS', marking their return in ship-owning.
Geared tonnage remained in demand, with one sale on the Supramax front, the INCE EGE (ABT 57K DWT, 2010, STX DALIAN) which has managed to achieve a price of high USD 12m from u/d buyers, basis surveys freshly passed and with the majority of this week's sales being Handysize tonnage. The most notable was the Japanese controlled OCEAN TACT (ABT 36K DWT, 2019, SHIKOKU, SCRUBBER) which has been committed at an unconfirmed price, as reports have emerged that she achieved USD 24 m, however others following the Vessel believe she has achieved levels in the 23's. Furthermore, the VEGA FALKTIND (ABT 32K DWT, 2011 FUJIAN MAWEI) has been committed to u/d interests for levels in the mid USD 8m while the one-year-younger ARAWANA (ABT 32K DWT, 2012, TAIZHOU MAPLE LEAF) is now committed for u/d levels, about three weeks after the sale of the sister-ship from the same Owners the NEPTUNE, which had managed to achieve levels of USD 9m at the time. Finally, one more smaller Handysize has been sold to Vietnamese buyers, the DANSHIP BULKER (ABT 28K DWT, 2009, I-S) for a price of USD 8.5m.
Tanker
Although this has been another busy week for tankers, there are only a couple of sales to report, with the most interesting one being the sale of the ‘Thenamaris' owned Suezmax tanker SEAVOYAGER (ABT 159K DWT, 2007, HYUNDAI) which is changing hands for levels of rgn USD 37.2m. Buyers are believed to be Chinese. On the smaller sizes, another MR tanker, the JAG POOJA (ABT 48K DWT, 2005, IWAGI) has been sold, achieving levels of USD 11.5m from undisclosed interests.
