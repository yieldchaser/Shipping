NEW BUILDING
In the gas carrier market, Daewoo (DSME) announced taking orders for three firm 200k CBM LNG Carriers from an unknown owner,
with deliveries expected to run throughout 2H 2025.
In containers, Hyundai Mipo announced contracting three firm 1,800TEU containerships from Clients of Euroseas, with the first vessel to be delivered
in 1Q 2024 and the second and third vessel in 2Q 2024. The yard also announced orders for two firm 1,800TEU vessels from Eastern Pacific
Shipping, with both vessels to be delivered within 4Q 2023. COSCO HI (Yangzhou) announced taking orders for two firm 700TEU Coastal Trading
containerships, with delivery expected October and December 2023 respectively.
RECYCLING
Spring Forward!
As we turn the clocks forward this weekend to edge closer to summer, market sentiment this week has certainly not sprung forward
and in fact, further negative corrections took place, this time from Bangladesh. There appears to be so much unpredictability around
the recycling industry lately as global commodity markets continue to drive the volatility in the market and causing recyclers, and cash
buyers, to seemingly take on a ‘wait and see' attitude.
We have finished the week with the Bangladesh market suffering a slight downward pressure, India and Pakistan stable with no real
change in sentiment, and the Turkish market continuing to look promising with historical numbers in the USD 400's being witnessed.
As yards begin to empty, due to the continuing lack of supply, we may see price levels rise once again from the Indian sub. Continent as recyclers
once again become frustrated by the small volume of tonnage arriving to their shores, provided of course, domestic steel prices can justify any
change.
