NEW BUILDING
In the gas carrier market, Hyundai HI (Ulsan) announced contracting eight firm 174k CBM LNG Carriers with an unknown European owner, with the
first four vessels set to deliver in 2025 and the second four within 2026. Hyundai Samho announced taking orders for two firm 174k CBM LNG
Carriers, also from an unknown owner, with both vessels expected to deliver in 2026.
In containers, Pacific International Lines (PIL) announced ordering four firm 8,000 TEU containerships at Yangzijiang Shipbuilding. The vessels will be
equipped with LNG dual-fuel propulsion as well as holding an "Ammonia Ready" Class notation and are set to deliver from the first quarter through
until the third quarter of 2025. Yazi Changbo (Part of Yangzijiang Shipbuilding group) announced that Clients of Cosmoship Management ordered two
firm 1,800 TEU Containerships at the yard, with both vessels set to deliver in early 2024. MPC Containerships announced ordering two firm geared
methanol dual fuel 1,300 TEU Containerships at Taizhou Sanfu, with both vessels expected to deliver in 2H 2024. It was reported that Wuchang
Shipbuilding took orders for four firm gearless 1,182 TEU Containerships from CA Shipping, with the vessels all set to be delivered in 2024.
In tankers this week, it was reported that Shanghai Jinghan Shipping ordered both a firm 7.3k dwt and a firm 3.7k dwt Chemical tanker at Taizhou
Wuzhou. The vessels will both be equipped with Stainless Steel Cargo Tanks and will deliver in 2024.
In the ferry market, it was reported that Casco Bay Lines ordered a 50M LOA Car and Passenger Ferry at Senesco. The vessel can carry 599
Passengers and will have hybrid electric propulsion, with the vessel set to deliver in 2024.
RECYCLING
Omelette without Eggs!
In what has been a gloomy couple of weeks for the market, coupled with a lack of tonnage and price levels alarmingly coming off, there is now a more
positive outlook in the past few days with steel prices rallying by nearly 15% locally. This has prompted end recyclers to put forward more prominent
enquiries and cash buyers quoting back closer to the USD 600 $/per ldt number once again for market tonnage. It is worth noting that we are still in a
precarious position due to the local currencies against the US dollar across all the Indian sub-continent areas and this could affect the market at any
time, with VLCC's being quoted 30/40 dollars below other smaller units due to the large outlay and lack of financing available to fund such large LDT
units.
Bangladesh once again feels the firmest market with mixed signals coming from Pakistan as to how aggressive they are for tonnage, especially as
cash buyers still feel wounded from the recent round of re-negotiations, they encountered from the waterfront in Gadani and may feel hesitant to
engage with the local end buyers.
Pricing is very difficult to assess however as the market needs a definitive sale so that we can start to accurately guide where pricing truly lies, but with
the firming freight markets in the Tanker sector over the past few weeks, it will be some time before we start to see a glut of tonnage for recyclers to
feast upon.
Finally, we would like to wish a very happy Eid Al-Adha Mubarak to our readers observing the holiday this weekend!
