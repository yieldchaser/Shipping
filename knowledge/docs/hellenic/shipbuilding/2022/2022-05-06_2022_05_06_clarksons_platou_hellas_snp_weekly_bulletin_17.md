Hellas S&P Weekly Bulletin 6 May 2022
NEW BUILDING
In tankers this week, clients of Navios Maritime Partners announced ordering four firm 115k dwt LR2 product tankers at K Shipbuilding, with delivery of
the first three vessels set for 2024 delivery and the final vessel in 1Q 2025.
In containers, it has been reported that Seaspan Corporation ordered eight firm 8,000TEU vessels at K Shipbuilding. The vessels will all be equipped
with LNG dual-fuel propulsion, with deliveries expected to run from 2Q 2024 through to 1H 2025.
RECYCLING
Eid Mubarak.
The various global holidays that transpired this week have set the tone for a slower week in relation to activity and negotiations.
Various holidays in the Far East and the Eid festivities commencing have this week ensured the biding table was relatively empty with the shortage of
tonnage in evident.
As the markets re-open in Pakistan and Bangladesh next week, the general feeling is that we may see price levels steadily rise again with Buyers
having a small opening between now and the upcoming Monsoon season early June. With the encouraging freight markets in all sectors remaining
