NEW BUILDING
In Tankers this week, Asiatic Lloyd declared their two optional 50k dwt MR's at Hyundai Vinashin, with delivery of both vessels expected in 4Q 2023.
In containers, Daehan announced taking orders for four firm 7,200TEU Containerships from Clients of Danaos, within 2024, starting from April. It was
reported that Celsius ordered two firm 3,000TEU Containerships at Penglai Jinglu. The vessels hold a "Methanol ready" class notation and will both be
delivered in 1H 2024.
In the Ferry market, Caledonian Maritime Assets (CMAL) announced ordering two firm 275LM RoPax's at Cemre Shipyard. The vessels are Battery-
Hybrid Fitted and will be delivered in 4Q 2024 and 1Q 2025 respectively. Cemre Shipyard also announced taking order for a firm Batter-Hybrid fitted,
399 Passenger Ferry for Torghatten Nord, set to be delivered in 2024.
RECYCLING
Holiday mode!
As we entered Ramadan this week, there was a subdued feeling across the sub-continent as end recyclers started to observe the holy month and
retreat home. This has been a familiar theme of a quiet market across sectors in all regions for most of the year with a distinct lack of tonnage
continuing to starve the ship recyclers. However there was an impressive price received for the below reported Capesize, which has been a rarity this
year with the firm freight markets on offer.
This price follows an announcement from the Central Bank in Pakistan that imposed a 100% cash margin on the importation on 177 items including
Steel products. By not including Ships in this new banking margin, prices are expected to increase further in Pakistan with this being one of the sole
ways to import Steel into the country with no cash restrictions. The Government seems to have taken this step to discourage imports and support the
balance of payments due to the struggling Pakistani rupee (PKR) which had depreciated to an all-time low of 185.4 against the dollar this week.
