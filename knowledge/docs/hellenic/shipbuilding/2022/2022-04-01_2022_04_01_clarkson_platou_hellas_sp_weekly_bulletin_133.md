NEW BUILDING
In tankers, Hyundai Vinashin announced taking orders for two firm plus one optional 115k dwt LR2's for clients of Metrostar Management, with
delivery set for within 2024, from the second quarter. Erik Thun AB announced ordering one firm 18k dwt Chemical Parcel Tanker at CMJL
Yangzhou. The vessel will LNG Dual-fuel with Ice Class 1A and will be delivered in 2024.
In dry bulk, CSSC Qingdao Beihai announced orders for two firm 210k dwt Newcastlemaxes for Bocimar NV. The vessels will have an Ammonia
ready class notation and will be delivered in 2024, bringing their series to ten at the yard. CSSC Dalian Shipbuilding announced contracting ten firm
coastal trading 65k dwt Ultramaxes for CITIC Financial Leasing, with delivery of the vessels set to run throughout 2024 and 2025. Nantong Xiangyu
announced orders for two firm 63.5k dwt Ultramaxes for Huaxia Financial Leasing, with delivery expected in 2H 2024.
In the gas carrier market, CSSC Dalian Shipbuilding announced taking orders for two firm plus two optional 175k CBM LNG Carriers for China
Merchants Energy Shipping, with delivery of the firm vessels expected in 2025. Hyundai Samho announced contracting two firm 174k CBM LNG
Carriers for an unknown European owner, with delivery of both vessels expected in 1H 2025. Samsung announced taking order for a firm 174k CBM
LNG Carrier for an unknown owner, with delivery slated for October 2025. MHI Shimonoseki announced contracting a firm 3.5k CBM LNG Bunkering
vessel for KEYS Bunkering West Japan, with delivery of the vessel set for March 2024.
In containers, Wan Hai Lines announced contracting five firm 13,100 TEU Containerships at Samsung, with delivery of the vessels expected to run
throughout 2H 2024. Regional Container Lines (RCL) announced ordering two firm 12,000TEU Containerships at Nihon Shipyard, with delivery of the
vessels slated for August and December 2024 respectively, this doubles their order to four at the Yard. Hyundai Samho announced orders for four
firm 7,600 TEU LNG DF Containerships for an unknown owner, with deliveries expected to run through 2H 2024. HJSC Yeongdo announced taking
orders for two firm 5,500TEU for MPC Container Ships, with delivery of both vessels set for within 1Q 2024.
RECYCLING
No April Fool!
All markets remain stable, especially now as the rates from Bangladesh have corrected back from the recent hikes to those on offer from their
counterparts in India and Pakistan. The domestic steel prices weakened again this week in Bangladesh which has brought about this turnaround and
the local recyclers are certainly not as aggressive when offering for available units. As such, recyclers will not be foolish, should a candidate become
available, as demand is certainly outweighing the supply once again.
Whilst it is difficult to see the USD 700/ldt being breached again (only a few units registered this), the lack of tonnage may tempt the recyclers to push
onwards once again, although the forthcoming Ramadan (commencing this week), could potentially slow activity/sentiment throughout April.
