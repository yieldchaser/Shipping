NEW BUILDING
In tankers this week, it was reported that CSSC Dalian Shanhaiguan took orders for two firm 115k dwt Aframax tankers from China Merchants Energy
Shipping, with both vessels set to be delivered within 2025.
In dry bulk, New Dayang announced taking orders for two firm 64k dwt Ultramaxes from AVIC Leasing, with both vessels expected to deliver within
2024.
In gas carrier market, Samsung announced orders for 12 firm 174k CBM LNG Carriers for an unknown owner, with deliveries set to run from 2H 2025,
with the final vessel to deliver in July 2026. The yard also announced contracting two firm 174k CBM LNG Carriers for a separate unknown owner,
with deliveries both expected in 4Q 2024. Kawasaki HI announced taking orders for one firm plus one optional 87k CBM VLGC for Kumiai Navigation,
with the firm vessel set to deliver in 1Q 2025.
In containers, Jiangnan announced contracting two firm plus two optional 14,000TEU containerships for their domestic counterpart BAL Container
Lines, with the vessels set to deliver within 2H 2025. Clients of Navios announced ordering two firm plus two optional 7,700TEU at HJSC. The vessels
will be equipped with LNG dual-fuel propulsion, with the firm vessels delivering in 4Q 2024.
RECYCLING
Motionless!
The stagnant market has continued this week which has once again seen price indications to weaken from the three recycling destinations in the
Indian sub. Continent.
The summer months have certainly kicked in and there is certainly nothing from the recycling industry to tempt parties away from their holiday dreams.
The interesting scenario is that there remains a distinct lack of tonnage yet, price levels sink further. Not exactly a happy medium!
Currency and commodity issues look set to hamper the sentiments of the recyclers for some time. There have been talks that the Bangladeshi market
has fallen by some USD 30-40 per ldt this week. Aside from the severe monsoon rains currently being experienced, for which our thoughts go to those
who are suffering at this torrid time, the shortage of U.S. Dollars internally is creating difficulties for any recycler to open a Letter of Credit for
transactions, particularly large ldt tonnage.
Across the way, India and Pakistan are not fairing any better with their own domestic currency issues obstructing any hint of a rebound. All in all, this
lifeless market looks set to persist throughout the summer months.
