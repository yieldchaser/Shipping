New Building
In tankers, Chongqing Chuandong announced taking orders for two firm 11.3k dwt Chemical Tankers from SC Shipping, with both vessels
expected to deliver in 2024. Lianyungang Shenghua announced taking orders for five firm plus five optional 7k dwt Bunkering vessels from
Singfar International. The vessels will be fitted with LNG dual-fuel propulsion with the firm vessels expected to deliver in 2023.
In dry bulk, it was reported that Yangzijiang took orders for four firm 32k Handysize Lakers from Navibulgar, with the vessels set to deliver in
2024. It was also reported that Jiangmen Nanyang took orders for four firm 40k dwt Handysize's from Ciner Denizcilik with the vessels expected
to deliver in 2Q and 3Q 2024. Oshima Shipbuilding announced contracting one firm 55k dwt Supramax for MOL Drybulk, with the vessel slated
for delivery in 2H 2024. The vessel is set to be equipped with a hard sail system which will harness wind propulsion and is the second ship in
the series.
Another busy week in the gas carrier market, as Hyundai HI announced contracting seven firm 174k CBM LNG Carriers for an NYK, K-Line,
MISC Berhad and China LNG Shipping Joint Venture. The vessels have been contracted as part of the Qatar Energy Project and with the first
three vessels to deliver in 2025 and the remaining four in 2026. DSME announced taking an order for a firm 174k CBM LNG Carrier from an
unknown Asian owner, with delivery of the vessel expected within 3Q 2026.
In containers, Yangzijiang announced contracting two firm gearless 4,600 TEU Containerships for their domestic trading customer Trawind
Shipping, with both vessels slated for delivery in 1Q 2025. It was reported that Namsung Shipping ordered two firm 32m beam, gearless, 2,500
TEU Containerships at Hyundai Mipo, with both vessels set for delivery in 2H 2024.
In the ferry market, it was reported that Sefine Shipyard contracted an LNG Ready, 1,500 Passenger, 290 Car Ferry for Caronte & Tourist. The
vessel is set to deliver in 1H 2024.
Recylcing
No Cohesion!
There have been some mixed feelings in the market this week with us finally having a market sale to benchmark off against. However, many
are expecting this to not be a recycling sale and whether the Buyer may trade further as some of the recyclers feel that the price is too high
and not supported locally. Therefore, there is a disparity and opinion on where the true market lies, with India being the only pure market
being able to import larger vessels due to the continuing Letter of Credit complications in Bangladesh and Pakistan, due to the well-publicised
U.S. Dollar shortages. The resultant factor is that only the smaller LDT tonnage realistically looks set to be imported into these two countries for
the time being.
Furthermore, with the global economic instability and currency fluctuations, Buyers feel reluctant to take on larger LDT tonnage. By the time
it takes to completely dismantle the unit, the market could be considerably lower and therefore they must look at hedging their future
position. What this means is that we may see a turnaround from the norm whereby a discount could be witnessed on large LDT units
compared to those smaller vessels.
We still expect to see a very subdued remainder of the summer. Holidays being observed around this weekend in Greece and Hong Kong,
as well as entering the final two weeks of August which traditionally is the main holiday period around the world, will see the recycling industry
starved of any major action.
