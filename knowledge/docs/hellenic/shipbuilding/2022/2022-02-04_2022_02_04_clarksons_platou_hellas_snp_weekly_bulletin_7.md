NEW BUILDING
In the gas carrier market, Daewoo (DSME) announced contracting two firm 174k CBM LNG Carriers from an unknown owner, with delivery of both
vessels expected in 2H 2025. Hyundai Mipo announced taking order for a 12.5k CBM LNG Bunkering vessel, with delivery set for March 2024. Sanyu
Kisen ordered a 1,450 CBM CO2 Carrier at MHI Shimonoseki, with delivery expected within 2H 2023.
In containers, Daewoo (DSME) announced taking orders for six firm LNG dual-fuel 15,600 TEU Containerships from an unknown European owner,
with delivery of the vessels expected throughout 2025. Hyundai Mipo announced two separate orders for 2,800 TEU Containerships, contracting four
firm vessels from an unknown owner with deliveries from end 2023 and run throughout 1H 2024, and two firm vessels from clients of Euroseas, with
the vessels expected to be delivered in 4Q 2023 and 1Q 2024.
In PCTC's, GSI Nansha announced taking orders for two firm 7,000 CEU PCTC's from H-Line. The vessels are LNG dual-fuel and will both be
delivered in 2025, bringing their series to four at the yard.
In the ferry market, CLdN (Coblefret) announced contracting two firm LNG dual-fuel, 8,000 LM Ro-Ro's at Hyundai Mipo. The vessels are battery
hybrid fitted and will both be delivered within 1H 2025. GSI Nansha announced taking orders for two firm 3,100 LM Passenger Ferries from Grandi
Navi Veloci, with vessels expected to be delivered in 2025.
RECYCLING
Escalating!
As the week moved at a snail's pace whilst the Far Eastern markets celebrated their Lunar holidays, price levels continued to jump northwards as the
current supply of tonnage to the market gives no satisfaction to the demand from the recyclers.
The Indian Union Budget for 2022-2023 was announced in India this week, the outcome of which fuels the positivity towards the recycling market
through the year as strong demand for steel looks set to remain. There has been a strong push for increase in productivity for domestic infrastructure
around business areas such as construction, railways and roads which should give an incentive for steel demand to remain firm throughout the year.
This week, India has witnessed improved indications on the back of the domestic steel market gaining momentum by some USD 30/ldt compared to
recent weeks.
Generally, however, the rates from India remain lower than their counterparts from Bangladesh and Pakistan as we now see price levels creep up
towards the mid USD 600 levels with room for improvement. The major surprise is that the lack of tonnage supply remains in place and Owners,
particularly from the tanker sector, are not being tempted, yet…, by these ongoing improvements.
