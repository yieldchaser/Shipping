New Building
In tankers this week, FureBear (Furetank and Algoma joint venture) announced ordering two firm 18k dwt Chemical tankers
at CMJL Yangzhou. The vessel's will be fitted with LNG dual fuel propulsion and Ice Class 1A, with the first vessel expected to
deliver in 2024 and the second in 2025.
In the ferry market, It was reported that Polish Baltic Shipping ordered a firm 1,000 Passenger, 3,000 Lane Meter Car and
Passenger Ferry at Navali Visentini. The vessel is set to deliver in 2H 2024.
Recycling
Desolate!
The ongoing saga surrounding the financial woes in both Bangladesh and Pakistan continues with no end in sight for the
foreseeable future. As such, there will be limited interest from these destinations should any large LDT unit become available
for a potential recycling sale (although any such vessel continues to be gobbled up by second hand buyers). Smaller units
should gain impressive numbers given the recyclers lack of available funds to finance larger tonnage, especially as there is
a requirement to attempt to fill their unoccupied yards.
India remains the only area from the Sub-Continent markets showing some stability. Domestic steel markets are improving,
and the Indian Rupee looks to have stabilised following the recent weakening against the U.S. Dollar which is giving some
encouraging signs. However, whilst the yards are becoming emptier, the flow of tonnage remains a trickle which will not
satisfy the current demand on the waterfront.
There has been much emphasis on the Capesize sector in media outlets over the last couple of weeks due to the rates having
adjusted below the USD 10,000/day, however whereas some anticipate this to suddenly push such tonnage into the recycling
market, we expect this sudden expectation to remain unfulfilled whilst some analysts predict a rebound in freight rates in the
latter part of this year. We do expect to see some price improvements for recycling, possibly post-monsoons, especially
factoring in the lack of tonnage arriving to the shores for recycling, however, the obvious question is whether the steel markets
will show signs of improvement to cater for any rise in price levels for ships for recycling. Everyone is certainly looking towards
China as to the uncertainty in their commodity markets and how their economy will evolve through to the end of this year
and into 2023.
