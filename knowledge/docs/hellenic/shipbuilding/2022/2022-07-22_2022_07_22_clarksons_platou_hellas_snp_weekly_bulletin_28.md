Hellas S&P Weekly Bulletin 22 July 2022
NEW BUILDING
In Dry, it has been reported that Foremost have contracted a pair of 185,000dwt Capesize bulk carriers at Namura Shipyard. Vessels understood to
deliver from 2H 2024 onwards against long term t/c to NYK.
In Containers this week, HMD have announced three firm gearless Bangkokmax 1,800 TEU Vessels for delivery in the second and third quarter of
2024, for which we understand was from their domestic liner company HMM. SAL Heavy Lift have announced ordering four firm plus two optional
14,600 dwt heavy lift MPP's from Wuhu Shipyard for delivery from the second quarter 2024. These Vessels will be equipped with 2 x 800T heavy lift
cranes.
Finally in Tankers, CSBC have announced an order from domestic CPC Corporation for single 50,000 dwt Product Carrier. The expectation is that this
will deliver within 2024. Xingtong Shipping have disclosed they have ordered a single 12,000dwt Chemical tanker at compatriot yard Taizhou Wuzhou
in China with the vessel due to deliver in end 2023.
RECYCLING
No Happy Medium!
We are trying to establish from where a concession will appear, the Sellers or the Buyers! On the outside we do not envisage many units to come for
recycling for the next month at least and therefore it would need the actual recyclers to increase their rates to tempt any Owner contemplating a
recycling sale.
However, financial restraints and weakening currencies against the U.S. Dollar continue to put cause for concern amongst the Indian sub-continent
recycling community and ensures no over-inflated levels at this current time. There are rumours abound of Letters of Credit unable to be opened for
larger LDT tonnage, particularly from Bangladesh, which further creates difficulties when and if a vessel of this ilk comes for sale.
We are now witnessing actual sales of tonnage for recycling fall to their lowest levels in a decade and certainly we cannot predict a massive change
any time soon during the summer months and heading into the final quarter of 2022.
