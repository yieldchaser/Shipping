Highlights of the Week
The 11th edition of the European List of ship recycling facilities has been adopted by the European Commission, encompassing a total of 48 yards. The updated
list comprises three additional yards situated in Turkiye. The Commission has further prolonged the expiration date for the inclusion of two yards listed in
Denmark and one yard listed in Italy. The present edition of the European List comprises a total of 38 yards located within Europe, encompassing the European
Union, Norway, and the United Kingdom, 9 yards situated in Türkiye, and 1 yard located in the United States of America.
The Indian government has opted to put on hold its initiative to double the nation's ship recycling capacity by 2024. Instead, it aims to formulate a fresh five-year
plan to revitalize the industry. This plan seeks to not only enhance the industry's capacity but also promote diversification, thus mitigating reliance on a singular
sector. In recent years, an unforeseen deceleration in the global economy, particularly evident in European nations and the United States, has coincided with a
significant reduction in capital investment by major shipping companies. Consequently, the implementation of governmental initiatives has been impeded,
resulting in a slower pace of execution.
In the first half of 2023, global crude steel production decreased by 1.1% year-over-year to 943.9 million metric tons, according to data from the World Steel
Association. China, the leading producer and consumer of this metal, increased production by 1.3% from January to June. For the fourth year in a row, China's
steel production in 2023 is expected to hover around 1 billion tons.
