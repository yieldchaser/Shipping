Highlights of the Week
A series of visits and high-level meetings have taken place in Bangladesh over the course of the last few weeks as part of a diligent and persistent effort. The
nation has ratified the Hong Kong International Convention for the Safe and Environmentally Sound Recycling of Ships as a result of these efforts. This result was
anticipated and has been addressed in our previous reports. The cabinet has approved the resolution, and the foreign minister will soon execute the ratification,
thereby facilitating the submission of the convention to the International Maritime Organization (IMO).
In an effort to battle inflation, the European Central Bank (ECB) has raised interest rates yet again. The European Central Bank hiked its key interest rate by a
quarter point to 3.5 percent, a day after the Federal Reserve suspended its own rate rises. It was Europe's eighth straight rise since July 2022, part of an
unprecedentedly quick effort by the bank to restrict the supply of credit to the economy as it strives to bring inflation to its target of 2% from 6.1%. The decision
was largely expected, and many experts foresee another quarter-point raise at the bank's next meeting on July 27.
The World Bank is the most recent organization to put forward a carbon tax on shipping ahead of the International Maritime Organization's (IMO) Marine
Environment Protection Committee (MEPC) meeting next month. According to a new report from the bank, a carbon tax on shipping alone might raise $40
billion to $60 billion per year between 2025 and 2050, money that could be used to accelerate the decarbonization of the shipping industry as well as improve
port infrastructure, particularly in developing nations. Furthermore, a portion of the proceeds should be directed to the least developed countries to aid in their
transition away from the usage of fossil fuels. The World Bank joins a rising chorus of governments and organizations, including the United States, France, and
Denmark, all eager for IMO member states to agree on and implement a carbon levy as soon as possible.
