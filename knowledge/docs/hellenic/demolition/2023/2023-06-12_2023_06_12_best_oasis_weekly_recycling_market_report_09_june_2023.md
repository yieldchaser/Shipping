post-pandemic rebound of 1,960 million tonnes in 2021, which was the largest drop since 2009 and 1991.
Oil prices increased and remained stable throughout the week due to apprehension regarding the growth of oil demand, which was offset by reductions in Saudi
output. Anticipated increase in demand during the summer holiday season in the United States, due to higher driving activity, is being counterbalanced by
concerns regarding China's sluggish fuel demand. Analysts anticipate a potential increase in oil prices in the event that the US Federal Reserve decides to forego
an interest rate hike during its upcoming meeting on June 13-14.
