Highlights of the Week
The ship recycling activities have thus far exhibited a sluggish pace, characterized by a dearth of transactions. The current situation has exhibited minimal
changes in recent weeks, primarily due to the prevailing monsoon bombardment and the diminished state of the domestic steel markets. Pakistan has
experienced a prolonged period of inactivity, while Bangladesh continues to grapple with an endless and increasingly difficult set of challenges related to its
Letter of Credit (LC) issues. India appears to exhibit a comparative disadvantage in terms of pricing, as it trails behind by a margin of 40 to 50 dollars.
Consequently, the level of activity continues to be constrained.
In view of Bangladesh's recent ratification of the Hong Kong International Convention, the foreign minister of Bangladesh has formally requested the Japan
International Cooperation Agency (JICA) for monetary aid to facilitate the establishment of a secure and environmentally responsible (Green) ship recycling
sector within the borders of Bangladesh. Furthermore, Bangladesh seeks the support of Japan in strengthening its candidacy for the position of Secretary
General of the International Maritime Organization, for the term spanning 2024-2027.
Global stock markets experienced an increase, while the value of the dollar strengthened, following an unexpected decrease in British inflation. This
development has contributed to a risk-averse sentiment in various markets, as investors anticipate that the Federal Reserve will raise interest rates for
the final time in the upcoming week. The value of the dollar experienced a rebound as market sentiment was bolstered by the unexpected decrease in
inflation in the United Kingdom during June. This decline brought the inflation rate to its lowest level in over a year, specifically at 7.9%.
