Highlights of the Week
Inflation-induced low end-user demand, the depreciation of regional currencies, and rising energy costs worldwide are all contributing to a bleak
outlook for ferrous scrap demand for this quarter. Global downfall is just beginning, and it is still unclear what it's effect on the ship recycling industry
will be. End-buyers across the major ship recycling destinations are in a wait-and-watch mode till a clear direction of steel demand and prices can be
ascertained from their domestic markets. Despite weak sentiments in the ferrous scrap market, the scarcity of units available for recycling has
ensured stability of prices being offered by the recyclers for securing the tonnage.
The European Council has adopted its eighth sanctions package against Russia for its invasion of Ukraine, which expands its ban on the import of
Russian steel products to include semi-finished steel, the European Commission said in an Oct. 6 statement. Among a number of other restrictions
announced, the EC said it was banning the export of coal, including coking coal, which was used in Russian industrial plants. Europe faces a high risk
of deindustrialization as soaring energy prices has forced many European corporations to reduce or halt production and shift investments to other
regions.
China's real estate sector, which accounts for more than a third of the country's steel consumption, has been squeezed by a liquidity crunch and
sliding sales, resulting in decreasing profitability of Chinese steel companies. As the nation grapples with the effects of the property market crisis and
President Xi Jinping's strict zero-COVID policies, leading economists expect growth to slow to 3.2% this year.
Global foreign-currency reserves are falling at the fastest pace on record as central banks around the world intervene to support their currencies.
While most central banks are still capable of keeping interventions going, emerging economies, particularly those with large dollar-denominated debt
balances have been hard hit and are struggling to pay for imports of essential items.
