MARKET SUMMARY
The EU Environmental Crime Directive (Directive (EU) 2024/1203) continues to draw
significant attention within the recycling sector following the formal inclusion of
unlawful ship recycling as a criminal offence. The directive is expected to increase
compliance scrutiny on recycling destinations and further strengthen the importance of
environmentally compliant recycling practices across the industry.
INDIA
The Indian market softened further this week, with local prices coming off while the
U.S. Dollar continued to strengthen, keeping overall sentiment under pressure.
Despite the weaker market, demand from buyers remains healthy due to the limited
availability of recycling candidates. For non-sanctioned vessels, particularly units not
associated with the dark fleet, there still remains a good chance of securing firm buyer
interest.
One recycling yard has reportedly received U.S. approval to recycle sanctioned vessels,
and we have also submitted our application, which is progressing through the
approval process. We remain hopeful of receiving the necessary approval soon.
With the ASRY-Priya Blue joint venture now in place in Bahrain, the yard has already
received its first vessel, a barge, for recycling. Please keep us posted on any suitable
recycling opportunities for Bahrain.
There are also market discussions suggesting that authorities may consider requiring
vessels to be Indian-flagged prior to recycling delivery. While nothing has been formally
confirmed, the industry remains concerned that such a move could discourage owners
from recycling in India and divert tonnage toward competing destinations.
BANGLADESH
Bangladesh buyers remain keen to acquire tonnage, although prices have softened
from previous levels.
Market participants are closely monitoring expectations of an increase in import duty
on scrap vessels, anticipated during the first week of June, resulting in a more cautious
approach from buyers on pricing levels.
Overall sentiment remains steady, although buyers are maintaining conservative
numbers until further clarity emerges regarding the proposed duty adjustments.

[Page 3]
PAKISTAN
The Pakistan market remains largely unchanged this week, with sentiment continuing
to hold firm.
Supply constraints continue to support the market, as limited scrap, HMS and
shredded material is being received from the Middle East, while fresh vessel availability
also remains limited.
Overall, the shortage of imported material and limited tonnage supply are keeping
price ideas firm, although buying activity remains selective.
TURKIYE
The recycling market in Turkiye remains relatively stable this week, although sentiment
continues to be affected by the depreciation of the Turkish lira, which has limited
buying appetite and kept activity for fresh tonnage subdued.
Most recycling yards, particularly EU-approved facilities, are operating at high capacity
levels, resulting in limited availability for prompt deliveries and a cautious approach
across the market.
Overall, market participants remain watchful of currency movements and yard
capacity developments, with sentiment expected to stay steady but cautious in the
near term.
