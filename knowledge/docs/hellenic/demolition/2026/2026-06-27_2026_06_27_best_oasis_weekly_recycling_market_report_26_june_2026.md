MARKET SUMMARY
INDIA
Market sentiment in India has weakened, with local ship recycling prices correcting
across all segments in line with softer domestic fundamentals.
Most recyclers continue to acquire tonnage despite operating at unprofitable levels,
primarily to maintain business continuity and keep yard operations running.
Labour shortages that disrupted yard operations over recent months have largely
been resolved; however, extreme heat conditions continue to significantly slow
production levels.
With production expected to improve once weather conditions normalize, the
resulting increase in steel supply could place additional downward pressure on local
prices amid persistently weak domestic demand.
BANGLADESH
The negative price correction witnessed last week has curtailed aggressive bidding
activity, with recyclers adopting a notably more cautious approach.
Breakers remain bearish in their outlook, anticipating further price declines in the near
term before re-entering the market aggressively.
Weak domestic steel fundamentals, characterized by declining steel prices and
subdued demand, continue to weigh heavily on recycling activity.
As a result, most recyclers have shifted to a "wait-and-watch" approach, preferring to
monitor market direction before committing to fresh purchases.
PAKISTAN
Market activity remained subdued during the week, with only three working days
limiting overall trading activity.
No significant changes were observed in pricing, demand, or recyclers' buying
sentiment compared to the previous week.
The market remains largely unchanged, with participants awaiting a full working week
for any meaningful developments.

[Page 3]
TURKIYE
Market sentiment in Türkiye remains soft, primarily due to lower mining raw material
prices, which have placed downward pressure on domestic steel prices.
Limited demand in the local steel market continues to restrict buying interest, keeping
recycling activity subdued.
Recyclers remain cautious as weak steel demand and softer pricing continue to
influence market sentiment.
