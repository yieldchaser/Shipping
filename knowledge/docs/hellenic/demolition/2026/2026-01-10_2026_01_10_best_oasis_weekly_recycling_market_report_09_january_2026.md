MARKET SUMMARY
INDIA
The market softened drastically by the end of the week, as sentiment turned sharply
weaker following fresh concerns linked to a potential 500% U.S. tariff threat under a
proposed sanctions bill targeting buyers of Russian oil.
The broader commodity complex also softened, with several commodities recording
sharp daily drops, further pressuring confidence.
Buyers are mainly looking to buy tonnage strictly at market level, but we do not see
much tonnage entering the market, keeping activity thin.
Despite the softer local mood, international scrap prices remain firm, supported by
Türkiye, which continues to underpin import levels.
BANGLADESH
The market remains subdued, with very limited movement throughout the week.
Buyer participation has become extremely thin, with only a few buyers genuinely able
to purchase larger tonnages.
These few active buyers are expected to take advantage of the weaker market
sentiment, keeping negotiations heavily in their favor.
Local prices have shown some momentum, but it is not drastic at this stage, and the
market remains largely range-bound.
PAKISTAN
Market sentiment is slightly better, with recyclers showing more willingness to engage
compared to last week.
Recyclers are mainly targeting bulker tonnage below the $400/lt level, keeping bid
levels disciplined.
The increase in international prices for re-rollable and shredded steel is giving local
recyclers more optimism, supporting the overall sentiment.

TURKIYE
Türkiye remained quiet this week as the market moved through the New Year holiday
period, resulting in limited reportable activity.
Price levels and sentiment remained unchanged, continuing to mirror last week's
position.
