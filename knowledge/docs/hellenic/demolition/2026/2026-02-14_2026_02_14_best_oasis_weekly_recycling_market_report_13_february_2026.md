MARKET SUMMARY
INDIA
The Indian market came off towards the end of the week, with sentiment pressured by
a firmer U.S. Dollar and a weaker Indian Rupee.
Reports around potential Russia-U.S. trade developments, including renewed use of
the U.S. dollar as a key trading currency, have further shifted market perspective and
may keep the dollar strong in the near term.
Tankers continue to flow into the recycling system, keeping India as the primary
workable destination for such units; however, fresh guidance from the Directorate
General of Shipping (DGS), India, may restrict sanctioned tanker arrivals due to stricter
requirements on flag, P&I cover, and class compliance.
BANGLADESH
The Bangladeshi market remained buoyant this week, with buyers continuing to show
aggressive interest. However, it remains to be seen how long this momentum can be
sustained, as yard capacity is gradually filling up.
With elections approaching, sentiment stayed cautious, as the outcome is expected to
influence near-term confidence and broader market conditions, particularly on the
financial side.
Notably, across all recycling destinations, availability of fresh recycling candidates
remains limited, and it remains to be seen whether buyers will continue to pursue
units at tighter margins to keep yard activity moving, or become more selective going
forward.
PAKISTAN
The Pakistani market remained firm this week, supported by selective buying interest
from a limited number of active buyers.
Activity continues to be concentrated among a few HKC-approved yards with the
necessary permissions to recycle vessels, limiting broader market participation. Due to
this restricted recycling base, the market cannot be solely relied upon for consistent
absorption of tonnage.

[Page 3]
Across all recycling destinations, the dearth of recycling candidates remains evident,
and it remains to be seen whether buyers will continue to transact at tighter returns or
pause until supply and pricing conditions stabilize.
TURKIYE
The Turkish market remained stable this week, largely mirroring last week's conditions
both locally and on import.
Overall sentiment and activity were steady, with no major developments to materially
shift market direction.
