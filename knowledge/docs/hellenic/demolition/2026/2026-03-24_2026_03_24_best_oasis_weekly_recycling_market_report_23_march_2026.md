MARKET SUMMARY
INDIA
The Indian ship recycling market remained at a standstill this week, with sentiment
coming under pressure from the extraordinary increase in the USD/INR index and the
lack of corresponding improvement in local steel prices.
Market participants are also closely watching developments surrounding the
government's ship recycling scheme, where active work on the broader structure
remains ongoing and a standardized process is expected to be introduced in due
course.
Overall sentiment remains negative; however, the scarcity of available tonnage has
continued to attract some keen buyers who may still be willing to pay reasonable
levels, even as profitability remains under question.
BANGLADESH
Activity in the Bangladesh ship recycling market improved this week, with buyers
showing increased urgency to secure vessels ahead of the upcoming budget and
particularly targeting units that can be delivered before the new measures take effect.
Prices have remained firm, although the local market has not fully moved in line with
the stronger levels being offered by buyers, reflecting expectations that market
conditions may turn more favorable in the near term.
Sentiment in the region has also been supported by the presence of a newly elected
business-friendly political environment, which has added a more positive undertone to
the market.
PAKISTAN
The Pakistani ship recycling market remained largely unchanged from the previous
week, with sentiment showing little movement overall.
Market activity was limited as Eid holidays continued over the past few days, with most
participants away from the market during this period.
Looking ahead, a clearer direction may begin to emerge in the coming week as normal
activity resumes.

[Page 3]
TURKIYE
The Turkish ship recycling market remained quiet this week, with little local activity
reported due to the holiday period.
Import levels, however, moved up by around USD 8 over the course of last week,
providing a somewhat firmer underlying indication for the market.
With participants expected to return after the holidays, there is potential for improved
activity and sentiment in the week ahead.
