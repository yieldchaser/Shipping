MARKET SUMMARY
INDIA
The Indian market recorded a modest correction this week; however, this should not
be viewed as a sign of stronger sentiment but rather a market adjustment following
an earlier high-priced vessel sale.
A key development this week was the recyclers' association directing yards to
introduce loading charges, while also asking recyclers to restore stricter payment
discipline, with mills expected to settle on a next-day basis or within a maximum credit
period of 10 days, after payment cycles had loosened during recent geopolitical
tensions.
While these measures are expected to improve liquidity for ship recyclers, the
additional loading costs and tighter payment structure could place pressure on
downstream buyers and mills.
As a result, the local market may face some short-term weakness in the coming week
as participants adjust to the revised commercial framework and assess its impact on
margins.
BANGLADESH
The Bangladesh market softened slightly this week, with local prices easing on a day-
to-day basis.
Despite the softer local market, interest remains visible for mid-sized assets, which
continue to be the preferred tonnage for buyers.
Bangladesh remains the highest-paying recycling destination compared to the other
major markets, supporting continued buyer interest.
PAKISTAN
The Pakistan market remained firm this week, with buyers actively looking for vessels
and overall sentiment staying strong.
Local market conditions continue to support prices, particularly due to a shortage of
available goods.
This shortage has added strength to buyer sentiment and kept the market relatively
positive compared to other destinations.

TURKIYE
The Turkish market remained quiet over the past week, with limited activity and very
few transactions reported.
Market sentiment continues to be cautious, while trading levels remain subdued.
Looking ahead, market direction remains difficult to predict due to domestic political
uncertainty, regional geopolitical tensions, and continued depreciation of the Turkish
Lira.
