UPEE FIRMS, POSITION REPAIRED
Pakistan's Week 20 has been defined by an unexpected configuration: while the Indian Rupee
Ru pee firms. broke to a fresh record low at 95.71 and the Turkish Lira held near its prior record weakness of
45.24, USD/PKR firmed marginally from the Week 19 close of 279.61 to approximately 278.58 at the
interbank level on May 14, with open-market quotes at 278.9 to 279.85. The PKR is now the only
sub-continent currency that has not made a new low since the early-April ceasefire. This
counterintuitive strengthening reflects the combination of the State Bank's 100 basis point rate hike
to 11.5%, sustained remittance discipline, and the marginal narrowing of the import-cover stress
that had defined the immediate post-CPI environment. The PKR has returned to operating as the
most stable major currency in the basin in level terms.
The State Bank's first rate increase in three years has now had a full operational week to transmit
Hi ke absorbs. through the FX market, and the early evidence is supportive rather than destabilising. The April
CPI print at 10.9%, up 360 basis points from 7.3% in March, with transport at plus 29.9% and
housing at plus 16.8%, remains the operative inflation reading and the most consequential macro
datapoint Pakistan has produced this year. Prime Minister Shehbaz Sharif's disclosure that the
weekly oil import bill has risen from USD 300 million pre-war to USD 800 million currently
quantifies the external account pressure that the SBP rate response was calibrated to absorb. The
May print, due in early June, will be the test of whether the hike has materially shifted the
trajectory.
Local steel plate prices at PKR 190,000 per ton remain unchanged from the Week 18 close, with the
Position holds. USD equivalent firming to approximately USD 682 per ton on the marginal PKR strengthening.
Gadani's absolute pricing position remains the firmest of the year, and the gap to India has
widened to approximately USD 255 per LDT, given the divergent currency moves. The Gulf
proximity premium that defined Weeks 16 through 18, and narrowed in Week 19 on the CPI shock,
has been substantially repaired by the rupee firming. Three HKC-certified yards remain
operational, with further certifications in the pipeline. The candidate pool aligned with Gadani's
capacity has not contracted. The structural position has recovered from the Week 19 narrowing,
though the operational constraint remains the same: the monsoon window has narrowed to
approximately two weeks, and no tonnage release has materialised in the basin.
