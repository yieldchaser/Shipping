MARKET SUMMARY
INDIA
Market sentiment in India remains subdued, with recyclers increasingly concerned
that prevailing price levels are not supported by underlying local market
fundamentals.
Despite weak domestic demand and soft steel market conditions, a pronounced
shortage of available recycling candidates continues to underpin pricing across all
vessel segments.
Ship recyclers remain active in acquiring tonnage at elevated levels, largely to sustain
yard operations and retain workforce capacity rather than on the basis of improved
market economics.
The disconnect between local fundamentals and offered prices persists, although
limited vessel supply is expected to prevent any significant correction in the near term.
BANGLADESH
Following several weeks of aggressive bidding activity, Bangladeshi recyclers have
stepped back from the market, resulting in a correction of approximately USD 25-
30/LDT across all vessel categories.
Prior to the national budget announcement, breakers actively secured inventory amid
concerns over the potential introduction of heavier taxation measures.
Budget-related fears have since eased, with the government implementing only a
modest VAT increase from BDT 1,200 to BDT 1,500, a significantly milder outcome than
initially anticipated.
While recyclers with open yard capacity remain interested in fresh acquisitions, buying
appetite is now being expressed at notably lower and more realistic price levels.
PAKISTAN
Market sentiment in Pakistan remains unchanged from the previous week, with no
major movement reported across pricing or demand.
Local recyclers continue to maintain the same stance as last week, with market activity
remaining steady but without any fresh developments.
The coming week may provide clearer direction, depending on fresh tonnage
availability and any new market developments.

TURKIYE
Market sentiment in Türkiye remains soft, with recycling prices continuing to trend
lower amid weakening demand.
The market has moved further away from the stronger levels witnessed earlier in the
year, reflecting a gradual deterioration in buyer confidence.
Recyclers are exercising increased caution and remain reluctant to commit
aggressively to fresh purchases until clearer market direction emerges.
