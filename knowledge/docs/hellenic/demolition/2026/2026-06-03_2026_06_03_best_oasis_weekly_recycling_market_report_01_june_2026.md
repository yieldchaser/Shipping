MARKET SUMMARY
This week, the shipping industry and broader maritime market attention remained
focused on Posidonia in Greece, a key platform for industry engagement, market
insights, and commercial discussions. Relevant developments and key takeaways from
the event will be consolidated and shared in the upcoming weekly update.
INDIA
The Indian market remained highly unpredictable this week, with prices holding
steady and overall sentiment staying soft amid limited activity.
Buyer appetite remains firm nonetheless, supported by a steady flow of vessels
arriving in India, including a notable share of dark fleet tonnage, while improved steel
availability has traders looking to tighten recycling payment terms.
End prices remain the same whether recycling a standard vessel or a dark fleet unit;
however, with dark fleet tonnage acquired at cheaper rates, conventional recyclers are
finding it increasingly hard to compete.
On the policy front, the Government issued its first-ever ship recycling credit note
under the new scheme, confirming the mechanism is now operational, a positive step
towards strengthening India's competitiveness as a recycling destination over the
longer term.
BANGLADESH
The Bangladesh market remained largely the same as last week, with conditions
broadly unchanged.
Following the Eid break, yards have resumed operations, though activity stays quiet for
now, with no sales reported and overall buying reduced.
Rates have softened alongside the slower pace of business, keeping the market
subdued at present.
That said, momentum is expected to build over the course of this week as operations
settle back into rhythm.

[Page 3]
PAKISTAN
With the market closed for the Eid holidays over the past week, no sales were
concluded and conditions held steady from before the break.
Buyers remain keen for fresh tonnage, though limited vessel availability continues to
constrain purchasing.
On the regulatory front, Pakistan passed new ship recycling legislation this week
advancing the Hong Kong Convention, introducing mandatory hazardous-material
surveys and certification for vessels arriving for recycling - a positive step for the
longer-term growth of the country's recycling sector.
TURKIYE
The Turkish market has remained relatively quiet over the past week, with limited
activity, few notable transactions, and cautious sentiment keeping trading levels
subdued.
Despite the current lull, visits and inspections at Turkish yards by several major
container operators offer some encouragement, raising hopes of larger recycling
projects and a pick-up in activity going forward.
Forecasting remains difficult, however, as internal politics, regional tensions, and the
continued depreciation of the Lira sustain a high level of uncertainty over future prices
and market direction.
