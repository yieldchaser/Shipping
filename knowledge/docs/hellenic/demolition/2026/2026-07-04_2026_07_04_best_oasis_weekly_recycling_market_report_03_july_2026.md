MARKET SUMMARY
INDIA
Market sentiment softened further through the week, with local ship recycling prices
continuing to trend downward amid persistent weakness in buying interest.
Discussions remain ongoing between Indian recycling yards and the European Union
regarding the inclusion of three Indian yards on the EU-approved recycling list, with
one of our yards among the applicants.
A noticeable influx of vessels from the dark fleet continues to arrive at Indian recycling
destinations, contributing to market attention despite otherwise subdued trading
conditions.
Overall, the market closed the week on a softer note, with limited fresh developments
outside the ongoing regulatory discussions.
BANGLADESH
The local market remained weak overall, with a brief improvement during the week
proving unsustainable as prices softened again toward the close.
Despite the broader market weakness, buyers have shown exceptionally strong
interest in small- and mid-sized tankers, with few transactions concluded at notably
aggressive price levels.
Demand continues to be concentrated on these smaller tanker units, while buying
appetite for other vessel types remains limited.
Overall market sentiment remains cautious, with recyclers closely monitoring price
direction before expanding their purchasing activity.
PAKISTAN
Market sentiment remained stable during the week, with vessel prices largely
unchanged from the previous week.
A few vessels arrived during the week, providing some activity in an otherwise steady
market.
Recyclers remain keen to acquire fresh tonnage and continue to show interest in
available recycling candidates.
Overall, the market remains balanced, supported by stable pricing and continued
buying interest.

[Page 3]
TURKIYE
The Aliaga market has steadied following the recent correction in prices, though
overall activity remains limited.
Most yards continue to operate with healthy inventories, reducing their appetite for
fresh tonnage, especially larger and higher-grade units.
The wide price gap with the Indian subcontinent continues to restrict Türkiye's
competitiveness for standard recycling candidates.
As a result, demand remains largely driven by regulatory considerations, with EU-
flagged and EU-compliant vessels still favoring Türkiye despite lower recycling returns.
