MARKET SUMMARY
INDIA
The Indian market showed some recovery last week, with renewed buying interest
offering a sense of improvement.
However, with Bangladesh continuing to pay notably higher prices, most sellers remain
inclined to divert tonnage there, leaving India with limited activity.
The arrival of sanctioned vessels at substantially lower levels could challenge the
competitiveness of other local yards in the near term.
BANGLADESH
The Bangladeshi market has softened slightly, though it continues to attract firm buyer
interest, especially for tankers, where sentiment remains relatively positive.
In contrast, bulk carrier prices have weakened further, reflecting a more cautious
approach among recyclers.
Despite the softer tone, Bangladesh retains a clear lead over regional competitors,
maintaining stronger price levels and consistent momentum.
PAKISTAN
The Pakistani market remains largely unchanged, with dull sentiment and limited
activity persisting for another week.
However, the government's plan to establish a new green ship recycling yard at Port
Qasim has drawn industry attention, as it could bring much-needed modernization,
environmental compliance, and renewed investor confidence to the sector in the
longer term.

[Page 3]
TURKIYE
Market sentiment and price levels have remained stable over the past few weeks,
showing no major movement in either direction.
The ongoing shortage of available tonnage has kept activity subdued, as limited supply
continues to restrict new transactions.
The Central Bank of Türkiye has projected year-end inflation at around 31%, indicating
that price pressures in the economy remain elevated.
