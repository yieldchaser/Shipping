MARKET SUMMARY
INDIA
The market showed a marginal improvement during the week, though buying activity
remained slow as fast USD/INR movements continued to affect sentiment and limit
pricing confidence.
Developments surrounding Venezuela added to uncertainty, as tighter enforcement
on sanctioned oil movements raised questions over tanker demand, charter rates, and
the potential impact on global tonnage availability.
With limited clarity on how these factors may evolve, overall sentiment remained
cautious and the market remained in a wait-and-watch mode.
BANGLADESH
The market remained weak during the week, with limited buyer participation amid a
lack of local demand.
Political conditions continued to weigh on sentiment, creating uncertainty and delaying
fresh buying decisions.
While the market is under pressure, it remains too early to confirm any price
correction, and participants are maintaining a wait-and-watch approach.
PAKISTAN
Ship recycling prices remained largely unchanged during the week, with the market
continuing to move in line with previous levels and without any meaningful shift in
sentiment.
Buying interest and activity remained limited, offering little indication of momentum or
near-term improvement.
In the absence of new drivers, the market continued to lack direction, maintaining a flat
and subdued tone.

TURKIYE
Import prices softened slightly during the week, while local pricing levels remained
largely unchanged.
Despite some movement on the import side, overall sentiment showed no material shift,
with market participants maintaining existing positions.
Activity levels continued without notable change, and the market remained subdued,
reflecting its typically low-volatility nature.
