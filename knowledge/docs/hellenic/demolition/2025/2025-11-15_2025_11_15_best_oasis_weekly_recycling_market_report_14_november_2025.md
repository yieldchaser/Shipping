MARKET SUMMARY
INDIA
The market witnessed a slight pickup at the very end of the week, but overall
conditions remain largely unchanged due to the strengthened U.S. dollar, which
continues to limit market movement.
Buying interest exists but remains restricted because of the ongoing shortage of
tonnage. Whatever interest does emerge is mostly at lower levels.
The continued inflow of dark-fleet/shadow-fleet units at significantly cheaper prices is
putting additional pressure on local markets, as these vessels are being turned around
quickly and pulling overall sentiment down.
The DASR is still pending, and recyclers, as well as other market participants-continue
to press the authorities for its release.
BANGLADESH
Market activity remains quiet with no meaningful change in sentiment. Conditions are
largely the same as in previous weeks.
Buyers remain interested in 8,000-10,000 LDT units, but availability is extremely
limited. Larger vessels are attracting less attention mainly because only one or two
buyers currently have the LC capability to pursue them.
Ongoing LC constraints remain the main hurdle for the market, restricting the ability of
most buyers to commit to new tonnage.
PAKISTAN
The Pakistani market shows no change and continues to lean on the weaker side.
Sentiment remains flat with no signs of improvement.
Buying activity is minimal, and no significant movement in pricing or demand has been
observed.

TURKIYE
Conditions in Türkiye remain unchanged from the previous week. The market continues
to follow its usual pattern, showing very little movement week-to-week.
Sentiment and activity remain the same as before, with no notable developments or
shifts observed during the week.
