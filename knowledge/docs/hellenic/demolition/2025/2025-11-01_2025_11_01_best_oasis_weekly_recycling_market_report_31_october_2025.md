MARKET SUMMARY
INDIA
The Indian market has finally shown a very slight sign of hope, indicating a possible
improvement after a prolonged quiet period.
However, the U.S. dollar has strengthened considerably, limiting any positive impact
this minor shift could have brought.
Overall, the market remains flat, with sentiment still cautious and activity levels
subdued despite this faint hint of optimism.
BANGLADESH
The market remains largely subtle, with limited movement observed over the week.
Overall activity levels remain moderate.
Apart from an isolated transaction involving the sale of a large-sized tanker at
unusually high levels, considered unsustainable under current conditions, sentiment
remains steady without major change in pricing or buyer interest.
Yards in Bangladesh have started receiving DASR certification, with more expected to
follow in the coming period.
PAKISTAN
The Pakistani market continues to struggle amid very weak demand and declining
activity levels.
Prices for shredded steel have fallen further, adding to the overall market pressure.
In addition, illegal imports from Iran are disrupting local market dynamics, further
weakening sentiment and limiting any prospects of short-term recovery.

TURKIYE
The market remains stable, with no notable changes observed compared to the
previous week.
Overall conditions are steady, reflecting the typical pattern of how this market tends to
respond and move.
Sentiment remains neutral, with both buyers and sellers maintaining a wait-and-watch
approach in line with past behavior.
