Highlights of the week
Ship recycling markets across South Asia and Türkiye remained quiet overall this week, with limited activity and sentiment still soft. Local pricing in most
destinations held at last week's levels, but the broader picture continues to show a lack of momentum, with buyers across the region maintaining a cautious
approach. In India, the market stayed steady with no downward pressure on local prices. However, buyers have slowed their activity, and the overall mood
has turned more watchful. The strong dollar and its impact on costs remain a concern, overshadowing otherwise firm pricing levels. Bangladesh showed no
improvement either, with buyers staying on the sidelines and unwilling to move higher on price. Their reluctance to adjust expectations has left the market
flat and momentum lacking, even as conditions remain unchanged week on week. Pakistan continued in the same weak tone as before. Prices are at last
week's levels, but sentiment is soft and there has been little sign of any buyer participation. The overall market there remains stagnant, with no indication
of a shift in direction. Türkiye mirrored this subdued regional trend. Activity stayed slow, with no visible movement in the market. Prices held at last week's
levels, though pressure for a possible decline is gradually building given the weak demand environment. Taken together, the region continues to face a
muted period, with buyers holding back and little urgency to move. Pricing has held steady for now, but without stronger demand or a shift in sentiment,
the outlook remains soft across all key markets.
On the global front, renewed US tariffs have reignited trade tensions, adding further uncertainty to market sentiment worldwide.
