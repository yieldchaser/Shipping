MARKET SUMMARY
INDIA
The market has firmed this week, with local steel sentiment showing a modest
improvement.
India-U.S. trade discussions are progressing, helping ease currency-related concerns.
Expectations for a stronger INR are improving, offering better liquidity for recyclers
already committed to purchases.
Overall sentiment is more positive than in the past two weeks. Buyers remain cautious,
but firm interest, especially for mid-sized vessels, indicates a healthier buying appetite.
BANGLADESH
Market conditions in Bangladesh remain subdued, with only a small number of
financially capable buyers still active and willing to commit to new tonnage. Demand
for smaller units is minimal, and although some appetite persists for mid- to large-sized
vessels, overall purchasing activity remains soft and selective.
Broader economic challenges across the country continue to weigh heavily on steel
consumption, limiting the ability of local mills to absorb additional tonnage. Even
during the winter season, which is traditionally associated with stronger steel demand,
mills and end users have shown no significant increase in consumption, highlighting
ongoing market weakness.
More mills are shifting to cheaper sponge iron imports from India, reducing reliance on
recycled steel.
PAKISTAN
The Pakistani market shows little change from previous weeks, with sentiment
remaining subdued and most buyers hesitant to engage due to the lack of direction.
Conditions have been stagnant for several weeks, and there are currently no strong
indicators pointing to a near-term shift in demand or momentum.

TURKIYE
As noted in previous updates, the Turkish market continues to show no change, with
conditions remaining exactly the same as in past weeks and no movement or fresh
activity observed.
There has been no improvement in sentiment or demand, and the market is simply
following its usual routine, with nothing new emerging to indicate any shift in direction.
